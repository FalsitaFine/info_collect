
Group A strep: A well-known strain (a type) of streptococcus (strep) <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13954">bacteria</a> that can cause <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5567">strep throat</a> and common skin problems such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3948">impetigo</a> as well as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11963">rheumatic fever</a>. 

Autoimmune reactions to strep have also recently been associated with a number of disorders, including guttate <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/psoriasis/article.htm">psoriasis</a> (cradle cap), 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11277">Sydenham's chorea</a> (a movement disorder related to rheumatic fever), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4610">obsessive-compulsive disorder</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11338">Tourette syndrome</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2399">autism</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2269">anorexia nervosa</a>.
Group A strep are also known popularly as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/necrotizing_fasciitis/article.htm">flesh-eating bacteria</a>.
