
Mycobacterium tuberculosis: The bacterium that causes tuberculosis. M. tuberculosis has unusually waxy walls, is slow-growing and among the most <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5235">recalcitrant</a> bacteria to treatment. The complete <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3580">genome</a> sequence of M. tuberculosis was published in 1998 revealing remarkably large proportion of its coding capacity devoted to producing <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15392">enzymes</a> involved in lipogenesis and lipolysis, the buildup and breakdown of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15383">lipids</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15384">fats</a>). For more information, see: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tuberculosis_tb_facts/article.htm">Tuberculosis</a>.
