
Gliadin: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=16842">glycoprotein</a> (a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6553">carbohydrate</a> plus a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6554">protein</a>) within <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3613">gluten</a>. Gliadin is found in wheat and some other grains, including oats, rye, barley, and  millet. People with  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/celiac_disease_gluten_enteropathy/article.htm">celiac disease</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/crohns_disease/article.htm">Crohn's disease</a>, and other conditions may be sensitive to gliadin in the diet. In these conditions, antibodies to gliadin can often be detected in the blood. See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3614">Gluten enteropathy</a>.
