
Heterochromatin: A genetically inactive part of the genome. Heterochromatin was so named because its chromosomal material (chromatin) stains more darkly throughout the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7107" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cell cycle</a> than most chromosomal material (euchromatin). There are two types of heterochromatin: constituitive heterochromatin and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31987" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">facultative heterochromatin</a>.
