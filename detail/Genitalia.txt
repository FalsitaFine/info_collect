
Genitalia: The male or female reproductive organs. The genitalia include internal and external structures. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8869" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">female internal genitalia</a> are the ovaries, Fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, and vagina. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20894" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">female external genitalia</a> are the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6191" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">labia minora</a> and majora (the vulva) and the clitoris. The male internal genitalia are the testes, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3280" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epididymis</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20863" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">vas deferens</a>. The male external genitalia are the penis and scrotum.
