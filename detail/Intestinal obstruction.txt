
Intestinal obstruction: Blockage of the intestine by 
infolding (intussusception), malformation, tumor, digestive problems, 
a foreign body, or inflammation. Symptoms can include crampy 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm">abdominal pain</a>, lack of ability to eliminate normal feces, and 
eventually shock. On examining the abdomen, the doctor may feel a 
mass. Abdominal X-rays may suggest intestinal obstruction, but a 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2425"> barium enema</a> may be needed to show the actual cause. Treatment 
depends on the cause of the obstruction. See also: 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/intussusception/article.htm">Intussusception</a>.
