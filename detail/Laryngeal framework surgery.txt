
Laryngeal framework surgery: A surgical technique designed to improve the voice by altering the cartilages of the larynx (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9019" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">voice box</a>), which houses the vocal folds (the vocal cords) in order to change the position or length of the vocal folds. Laryngeal framework surgery is also called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12908" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">thyroplasty</a>.
