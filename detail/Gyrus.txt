
Gyrus: A convolution on the surface of a cerebral hemisphere caused by the infolding of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11490" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cerebral cortex</a>. The gyri are bounded by crevices in the cortex called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32260" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">sulci</a>. Many gyri are constant enough in their occurrence as anatomic features that they have been given names as, for example, the cingulate gyrus, the dentate gyrus, and so on. From the Greek gyros = a circle.
