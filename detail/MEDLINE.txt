
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8595" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">MEDLINE</a>: MEDLINE is the best known database of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8598" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">U.S. National Library of Medicine (</a><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6833" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">NLM</a>). The NLM is the largest medical <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4155" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">library</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> the world. It is part of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9786" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">National Institutes of Health</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4565" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">NIH</a>) in Bethesda, Maryland. It collects materials in all areas of biomedicine and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3663" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">health</a> care, as well as works on biomedical aspects of technology, the humanities, and the physical, life, and social sciences. NLM is a key resource for health <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13754" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">science</a> libraries and for all of medicine.
