
Nephroptosis: A condition in which the kidney descends more than the length of 2 vertebral bodies (or more than 5 cm) when a person moves from a lying down to upright position.  Its cause is unknown, but it often occurs in young, thin women. When symptoms are present, these include lower abdominal or flank <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pain_management/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pain</a> that is worsened by prolonged standing and relieved by lying down. It can also cause symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/nausea_and_vomiting/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">vomiting</a> and acute <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a> due to acute obstruction or ischemia of the kidney.  Also known as floating kidney or rental <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ptosis/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">ptosis</a>.
