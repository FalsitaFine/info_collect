
Blood pH: The acidity or alkalinity of blood. The pH of any fluid is the measure of the hydrogen ion (H<sup>-</sup>) concentration. A pH of 7 is neutral. The lower the pH, the more acidic the blood. A variety of factors affect blood pH including what is ingested, vomiting, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a>, lung function, endocrine function, kidney function, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/urinary_tract_infection/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">urinary tract infection</a>. The normal blood pH is tightly regulated between 7.35 and 7.45.
