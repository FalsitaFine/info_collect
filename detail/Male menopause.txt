
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/male_menopause/article.htm">Male menopause</a>: A controversial concept reflecting the belief that the decline in testosterone levels observed in men as they age causes a clinically significant and potentially treatable set of symptoms, including sexual dysfunction, mood changes, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/weight_gain/symptoms.htm">weight gain</a>, fatigue, and other nonspecific symptoms. While testosterone therapy has been recommended to treat men with midlife symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/low_testosterone_low_t/article.htm">low testosterone</a> levels, the value of male hormone therapy remains controversial because there are few long-term studies about the effects or benefits of testosterone supplementation.
