
Candidiasis: Disease caused by the yeast Candida albicans. Candida albicans can cause vaginal yeast infections, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diaper_rash/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diaper rash</a>, skin rashes that emerge in moist, warm folds of skin, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/thrush/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">thrush</a> (white patches inside the mouth and throat). Candidiasis tends to develop when the normal balance of bacteria is upset, as sometimes occurs with the use of antibiotics. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prevention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Prevention</a> measures include the use of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/probiotics/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">probiotics</a>, and in some cases, dietary changes. Candidiasis can be treated with antifungal medications. Candidiasis is usually a minor and easily addressed problem, but it can be an important problem for those with immune-system disorders, such as AIDS.
