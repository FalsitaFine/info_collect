
Hemolytic disease of the newborn: Abnormal breakup 
of red blood
cells in the fetus or newborn. This is usually due to 
antibodies made by the mother
directed against the baby's red cells. It is typically caused by 
Rh incompatibility,
that is differences between the Rh blood group of the mother and 
baby. Severe hemolytic disease can cause <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/anemia/article.htm">anemia</a> and heart failure. 
Less-severe cases include <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/jaundice_in_adults/article.htm">jaundice</a>, and can lead to brain damage is 
left untreated. Also known as erythroblastosis.
