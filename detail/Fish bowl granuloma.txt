
Fish bowl granuloma: Localized nodular skin
inflammation (small reddish raised areas of skin) caused by a bacterium called
 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/mycobacterium_marinum/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">mycobacterium marinum</a>. Fish bowl granuloma is typically acquired by occupational or
recreational exposure to salt or fresh water, often resulting from minor trauma during
caring for aquariums. The diagnosis is suggested by the history of exposure and confirmed
by culturing tissue specimens which yield the microscopic organism, mycobacterium marinum.
The infection can be treated with a variety of antibiotics, including <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/doxycycline/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">doxycycline</a>,
minocycline, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/clarithromycin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">clarithromycin</a>, rifampin, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/trimethoprim/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">trimethoprim</a>-<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sulfamethoxazole_smz_with_phenazopyridine-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">sulfamethoxazole</a>. Also called
"<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/swimming/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">swimming</a> pool granuloma."
