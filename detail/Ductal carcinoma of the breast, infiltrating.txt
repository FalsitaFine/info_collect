
Ductal carcinoma of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm">breast</a>, infiltrating: Infiltrating ductal carcinoma is one of several recognized specific patterns of cancer of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm">breast</a>. It is so named because it begins in the cells forming the ducts of the breast. It is the most common form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_facts_stages/article.htm">breast cancer</a>, comprising 65-85% of all cases.
