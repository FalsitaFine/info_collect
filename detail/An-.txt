
An-:   Prefix very much employed in medicine and all of the health sciences, indicating "not, without, or -less." 
For example, the prefix "an" figures into these words: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15491">anemia</a> 
(without blood), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=16199">anophthalmia</a> (no eye), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6599">anotia</a> (no ear), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2271">anoxia</a> (no oxygen). 

The "an-" usually becomes "a-"  before a consonant as, for example, in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6584">alexia</a> 
(not read), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6606">aphagia</a> (not eat), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2305">aphonia</a> (not voice, voiceless). 

The prefix "an-" comes from the Greek meaning "not."
