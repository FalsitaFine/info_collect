
Magnesium sulfate: A remarkably versatile compound administered intramuscularly and intravenously as an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20523" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anticonvulsant</a> and as a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5808" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tocolytic agent</a> (to halt premature labor), taken by mouth as a fast-acting laxative, and applied locally as an anti-inflammatory. Informally called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25455" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mag sulfate</a>. Also known as Epsom salt.
