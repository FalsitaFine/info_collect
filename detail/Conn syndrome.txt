
Conn syndrome: Overproduction of the hormone aldosterone by a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a> in the outer portion (cortex) of the adrenal gland. The excessive aldosterone results in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/low_potassium_hypokalemia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">low potassium</a> levels (hypokalemia), underacidity of the body (alkalosis), muscle weakness, excessive thirst, excessive urination, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/high_blood_pressure_hypertension/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">high blood pressure</a>. Also known as aldosteronism and hyperaldosteronism.
