
Aarskog-Scott syndrome: A syndrome of wide spaced eyes (ocular hypertelorism), front-facing (anteverted) nostrils, a broad upper lip, a malformed ("saddle-bag") scrotum, and laxity of the ligaments resulting in bending back of the knees (genu recurvatum), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3474" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">flat feet</a>, and overly extensible fingers. There are X-linked and autosomal forms of the disease. The gene for the X-linked form has been mapped to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18598" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chromosome band</a> Xp11.21 and identified as the FGD1 gene.
