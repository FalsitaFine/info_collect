
Enzyme, Warburg's yellow: A key respiratory enzyme discovered by the German biochemist Otto Heinrich Warburg (1883-1970), a pioneer in research on the respiration of cells and the metabolism of tumors. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7149" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Warburg's yellow enzyme</a> is a flavoprotein that catalyzes an oxidation-reduction reaction that is necessary for normal breathing, or use of oxygen,  of cells.
