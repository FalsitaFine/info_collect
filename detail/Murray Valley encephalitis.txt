
Murray Valley encephalitis:  A viral encephalitis first recognized in the Murray Valley, in Victoria, Australia, caused by a flavivirus that infects birds is transmitted by mosquitoes. Children are the most seriously affected. Patients may have <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm">headache</a>, fever, malaise, drowsiness or convulsions. The Murray Valley encephalitis virus is a flavivirus. See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=38469">Flavivirus encephalitis</a>.
