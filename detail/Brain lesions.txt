
Brain lesions: an anatomical abnormality of any part of the brain. A brain lesion may be due to trauma or any disease that can cause inflammation, malfunction, or destruction of a brain cells or brain tissue. A lesion may be localized to one part of the brain or may be widespread. Examples of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/brain_lesions_lesions_on_the_brain/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">brain lesions</a> include head injuries, tumors, vascular malformations, damage due to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stroke_symptoms_and_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">stroke</a>, and damage due to inflammation resulting from infections or chronic diseases.
