
Basophil: A type of white blood cell (leukocyte) with coarse, bluish-black granules of uniform size within the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2907" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cytoplasm</a>. Basophils are so named because their cytoplasmic granules stain with basic dyes. Basophils normally constitute 0.5 to 3 percent of the peripheral blood <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4145" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">leukocytes</a>, and contain histamine and serotonin. Also known as a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33465" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">basophilic leukocyte</a>.
