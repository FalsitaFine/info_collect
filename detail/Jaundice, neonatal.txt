
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4070" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Jaundice</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4520" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">neonatal</a>: Yellowish staining of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7901" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">skin</a> and whites of the newborn's eyes (sclerae) by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/stroke_symptoms_and_treatment/article.htm9" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pigment</a> of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2459" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bile</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2462" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bilirubin</a>). <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">In</a> newborn babies a degree of jaundice is normal. It is due to the breakdown of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5260" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">red blood cells</a>, which release bilirubin into the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2483" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">blood</a>, and to the immaturity of the newborn's <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4179" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">liver</a>, which cannot effectively metabolize the bilirubin and prepare it for excretion into the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5915" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">urine</a>. Normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6435" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">neonatal jaundice</a> typically appears between the 2nd and 5th days of life and clears with time.
