
Juxtaglomerular apparatus: The prefix "juxta-" comes from the Latin preposition meaning near, nearby, close.   The juxtaglomerular apparatus is a collective term referring to the cells near a structure called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=19009" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glomerulus</a> in the kidney. The juxtaglomerular cells are specialized cells that stimulate the secretion of the adrenal hormone aldosterone and play a major role in renal autoregulation, the kidney's self-governance.
