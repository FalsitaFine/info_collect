
Intravenous immunoglobulin: A sterile solution of concentrated antibodies extracted from healthy people that is administered directly into a vein. Abbreviated IVIG. IVIG is used to treat disorders of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3907" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immune system</a> or to boost the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3906" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immune response</a> to serious illness. Also known as intravenous <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=21065" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">gamma globulin</a> (IGG).
