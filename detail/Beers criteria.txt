
Beers criteria: Criteria for safe medication use in older adults -- for people over 65 years of age. First issued in 1991, these criteria have been repeatedly revised and updated. In addition to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/benzodiazepines_sleep-inducing-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">benzodiazepines</a>, the Beers criteria (or list) has included <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/amiodarone/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">amiodarone</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/amitriptyline/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">amitriptyline</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cimetidine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">cimetidine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/clonidine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">clonidine</a>, disopyramide, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/indomethacin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">indomethacin</a>, ketorolac, meperidine, methyldopa, and many antihistamines, antispasmodics, and muscle relaxants. Named for Dr. M.H. Beers, principal author of the original 1991 criteria.
