
Diverticulitis, treatment of acute: Antibiotics are  usually needed. Oral antibiotics are sufficient when symptoms are mild. Liquid or low   <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fiber/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">fiber</a> foods are advised during acute <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diverticulosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diverticulitis</a> attacks. In severe diverticulitis with  high <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a> , pain and distension, patients are hospitalized and given intravenous antibiotics. Surgery  is needed for persistent bowel obstruction or abscesses not responding to antibiotics.
