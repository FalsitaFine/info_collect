
Impaired glucose tolerance: A transition phase between normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glucose/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">glucose</a> tolerance and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a>, also referred to as prediabetes. In impaired glucose tolerance (IGT), the levels of blood glucose are between normal and diabetic. People with IGT do not have diabetes. Each year, only 1-5% of people whose test results show IGT actually develop diabetes. And with retesting, as many as half of the people with IGT have normal oral <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glucose_tolerance_test/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">glucose tolerance test</a> results. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/weight_loss/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">Weight loss</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">exercise</a> may help people with IGT return their glucose levels to normal.   With diet and exercise as many as 60% will not developed diabetes or it will be considerably slowed in its development.   See also: Oral glucose tolerance test
