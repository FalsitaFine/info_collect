
Amino acid: One of the20 building blocks from which proteins are assembled. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22828" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Isoleucine</a>, leucine, lysine, phenylalanine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22834" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">threonine</a>, tryptophan, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22837" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">valine</a> are deemed 'essential' <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39555" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amino acids</a> because the human body cannot make them and they must be obtained in the diet. Amino acids are sometimes taken orally in supplement form.
