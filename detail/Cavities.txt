
Cavities: Holes or structural damage in the two outer layers of a tooth  called the enamel and the dentin. The enamel is the outermost white hard surface and the  dentin is the yellow layer just beneath enamel. Both layers serve to protect the inner  living tooth tissue called the pulp, where blood vessels and nerves reside. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cavities/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Cavities</a> are caused by bacteria in the mouth. Small cavities may not cause pain, and may be unnoticed by the patient. Larger cavities can collect food, and the inner pulp of the affected tooth can become irritated by bacterial toxins, foods  that are cold, hot, sour, or sweet - causing <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/toothache_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">toothache</a>. Also referred to as caries.
