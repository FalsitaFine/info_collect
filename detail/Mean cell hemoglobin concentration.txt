
Mean cell hemoglobin concentration: The average concentration of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hemoglobin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">hemoglobin</a> in a given volume of blood. The MCHC is a calculated value derived from the measurement of hemoglobin and the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hematocrit/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">hematocrit</a>. (The hemoglobin value is the amount of hemoglobin in a volume of blood while the hematocrit is the ratio of the volume of red cells to the volume of whole blood.) The normal range for the MCHC is 32 - 36%. The MCHC is a standard part of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/complete_blood_count/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">complete blood count</a>.
