
ERT (estrogen replacement therapy): a form of therapy with estrogen hormones most commonly used to treat the symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/menopause/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">menopause</a>. It reduces or stops the short-term changes of menopause such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hot_flashes/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hot flashes</a>, disturbed <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sleep/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sleep</a>, and  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/vaginal_dryness_and_vaginal_atrophy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">vaginal dryness</a>. Estrogen replacement therapy is thought to help prevent <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/osteoporosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">osteoporosis</a>, a consequence of lowered estrogen levels. Estrogen therapy has more recently been referred to without the use of the term "replacement" and is simply referred to as estrogen therapy or estrogen therapy.
