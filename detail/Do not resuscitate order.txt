
Do not resuscitate order: A request not to have <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/human_immunodeficiency_virus_hiv/article.htm2" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cardiopulmonary resuscitation</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2855" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">CPR</a>) done if the heart stops or breathing ceases. One can use an advance directive form to make this request or one can tell the doctor to put a DNR order in the medical record. DNR orders are accepted by doctors and hospitals in most jurisdictions, including all states in the U.S.
