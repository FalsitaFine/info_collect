
Medical College Admissions Test (MCAT): The MCAT is a test that is required of all applicants to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=30954" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">medical school</a> in the U.S. and Canada. The MCAT is "a standardized test used to assess applicants' <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13754" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">science</a> knowledge, reasoning, and communication and writing skills." The MCAT is given under the aegis of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8733" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Association of American Medical Colleges</a>.
