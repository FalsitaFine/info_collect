In dentistry, the contact of the occlusal surfaces of the teeth.3. In <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2237" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anatomy</a>, a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4074" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">joint</a> (an area where two bones are attached for the purpose of motion of body parts). An articulation, or joint, is usually formed of fibrous <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2824" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">connective tissue</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2644" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cartilage</a>. Joints are grouped according to their motion: a ball and socket joint; a hinge joint; a condyloid joint (a joint that permits all forms of angular movement except axial rotation); a pivot joint; gliding joint; and a saddle joint.
