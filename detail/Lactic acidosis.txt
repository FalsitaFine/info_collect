
Lactic acidosis: Acidosis (too much acid in the body) due to the buildup of lactic acid in the body. Lactic acidosis occurs when cells make lactic acid (from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glucose/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">glucose</a>) faster than it can be metabolized. The key signs of lactic acidosis include unusually deep and rapid breathing, vomiting, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a>.   Lactic acidosis is a feature of different disorders, usually indicating a metabolic derangement in the acid-base system of the body.  Sometimes associated with severe infection, and can be a rare side effect of some medications such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/metformin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">metformin</a> (Glucophage).
