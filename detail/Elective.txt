
Elective: In medicine, something chosen (elected). An elective procedure is one that is chosen (elected) by the patient or physician that is advantageous to the patient but is not urgent.  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=14367" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Elective surgery</a> is decided by the patient or their doctor. The procedure is seen as beneficial but not absolutely essential at that time.
