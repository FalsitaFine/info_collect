
Human gene map: The locations of the human genes. The location of each gene is called a locus (plural: loci). The total number of known gene loci on the human gene map as of June 24, 2000 was 6270. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31292" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Chromosome 1</a> contained the most gene loci, namely 627, while the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6049" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Y chromosome</a> had the fewest, namely 24.
