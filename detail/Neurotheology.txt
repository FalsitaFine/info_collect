
Neurotheology: The search for the place(s) in the brain where religious beliefs originate. Neurotheology mixes terms and methods from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13754" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">science</a> and religion in an attempt to confer the authority of science upon religion.
For example, a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18450" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">SPECT scan</a>-study of a small number of Tibetan Buddhists and Franciscan nuns before and at the peak of their transcendent feelings was said to show a change in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4782" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">parietal lobe</a> of the brain.
