
Epi-: Prefix taken from the Greek that means "on, upon, at, by, near, over, on top of, toward, against, among." As in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3271" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epicanthal fold</a> (a fold of skin that comes down across the inner angle, the canthus, of the eye; <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9134" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epicardium</a> (a layer of fibrous tissue that surrounds the heart and the roots of the great blood vessels); <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9883" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">episclera</a> (a thin membrane on top of the sclera, the white of the eye); <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3282" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epidural anesthetic</a> (an anesthetic injected into the epidural space surrounding the fluid-filled sac, called the dura, around the spine which partially numbs the abdomen and legs); etc.
