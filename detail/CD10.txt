
CD10: A cell surface <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3266">enzyme</a> with neutral metalloendopeptidase activity. CD10 is also known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39567">CALLA</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39568">common acute lymphocytic leukemia antigen</a>). It serves as a marker for the common form of ALL (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13663">acute lymphocytic leukemia</a>) as well as for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2553">Burkitt lymphoma</a> and follicular germinal center lymphoma. CD10/CALLA is normally present on the surface of early lymphoid cells as well as on a number of other types of normal cells, such as especially cells in the kidney.
