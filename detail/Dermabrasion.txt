
Dermabrasion: A surgical procedure that involves the controlled scraping away of the upper layers of the skin by using sandpaper or some other mechanical means. The purpose of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dermabrasion/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">dermabrasion</a> is to smooth the skin and, in the process, remove small <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/scars/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">scars</a> (as from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/acne/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">acne</a>), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/moles/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">moles</a> (nevi), tattoos, or fine <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/wrinkles/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">wrinkles</a>. Dermabrasion is performed by a dermatologist. Chemical skin peels are an alternative to dermabrasion.
