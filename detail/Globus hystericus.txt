
Globus hystericus:  The sensation of having a lump in the throat when there is nothing there. Sometimes simply called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9022">globus</a>. 
Globus hystericus is a symptom of some physical disorders such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/reflux_laryngitis/article.htm">reflux laryngitis</a> as well as a classic sign of hysterical neurosis, a psychosomatic disorder characterized by a change or loss of physical function (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blurred_vision/symptoms.htm">blurred vision</a> or paralysis of the legs) that suggests a physical disorder but instead is an expression of a psychological conflict or need. 
<i>Globus</i> is a Latin word meaning, not surprisingly, a globe or sphere. The adjective is globoid or spheroid.
