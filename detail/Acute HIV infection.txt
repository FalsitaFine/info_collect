
Acute HIV infection: The body's initial reaction to infection by the HIV virus. Acute HIV infection is a flu-like syndrome that occurs immediately after a person contracts HIV (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/human_immunodeficiency_virus_hiv/article.htm">human immunodeficiency virus</a> 1, the agent that causes AIDS). The syndrome is characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm">fever</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sore_throat_pharyngitis/article.htm">sore throat</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm">headache</a>, skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm">rash</a> and swollen glands (lymphadenopathy).
