
Neanderthal: An extinct subspecies of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=40191" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Homo sapiens</a> who lived in Europe and the area around the Mediterranean from 100,000 to 30,000 years ago. Study of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8921" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mitochondrial DNA</a> indicates that Neanderthal man made little or no contribution to the genome of modern humans. Named after Neanderthal (Neander valley) near Dusseldorf, where bones of a Neanderthal man were first discovered in 1856.
