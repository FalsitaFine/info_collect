
Congenital lymphedema: A condition present at birth in which excess fluid called lymph collects in tissues and causes swelling (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/edema/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">edema</a>) in them. Congenital <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/lymphedema/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">lymphedema</a> is due to a congenital malformation (that is, a birth defect) of the lymphatic system. Congenital lymphedema can be found associated with the Noonan and Turner syndromes and a number of forms of lymphedema are clearly due to  genetic factors.
