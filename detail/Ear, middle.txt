
Ear, middle: A part of the ear that consists of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3177" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">eardrum</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5871" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tympanic membrane</a>) and, beyond it, a cavity (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5873" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tympanum</a>). This cavity is connected to the pharynx (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm7" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">nasopharynx</a>) via a canal known as the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8525" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Eustachian tube</a>. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7867" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">middle ear</a> cavity also contains a chain of three little bones, the ossicles (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4264" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">malleus</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25699" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">incus</a>, and stapes), which connect the eardrum to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7218" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">internal ear</a>. The middle ear communicates with the pharynx, equilibrates with external pressure, and transmits the eardrum vibrations to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7210" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">inner ear</a>.
