
Beta cell, pancreatic: A type of 
cell in the pancreas (the organ of the digestive system 
located behind the stomach). Within the pancreas, the beta 
cells are located in areas called the islets of Langerhans. 
They constitute the predominant type of cell in the islets. 
The beta cells are important because they make <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/insulin/article.htm">insulin</a>. 
Degeneration of the beta cells is the main cause of type I 
(insulin-dependent) <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm">diabetes mellitus</a>.
