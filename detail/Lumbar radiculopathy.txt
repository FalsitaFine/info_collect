
Lumbar radiculopathy: Nerve irritation caused by damage to the discs between the vertebrae. Damage to the disc occurs because of degeneration ("wear and tear") of the outer ring of the disc, traumatic injury, or both. As a result, the central softer portion of the disc can rupture (herniate) through the outer ring of the disc and abut the spinal cord or its nerves as they exit the bony spinal column. This rupture is what causes the commonly recognized pain of "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sciatica/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sciatica</a>" that shoots down the leg.
