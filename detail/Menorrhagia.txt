
Menorrhagia: Excessive uterine bleeding occurring at the expected intervals of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4355">menstrual periods</a>. The bleeding from the uterus starts on schedule but is heavier than usual and may last longer than usual. Menorrhagia may be a sign of an underlying disorder, such as hormone imbalance, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/endometriosis/article.htm">endometriosis</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/uterine_fibroids/article.htm">uterine fibroids</a> or, rarely, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm">cancer</a> of the uterus. Menorrhagia may cause significant <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/anemia/article.htm">anemia</a>. See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4351">Menometrorrhagia</a>; <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4367">Metrorrhagia</a>.
