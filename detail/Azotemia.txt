
Azotemia: A higher-than-normal blood level of urea or other nitrogen-containing compounds. The hallmark test for azotemia is the serum <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2492" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">blood urea nitrogen</a> (BUN) level. Azotemia is usually caused by the inability of the kidneys to excrete these compounds.
