
Giardia:  A genus of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5091">protozoa</a> that infect the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25976">gastrointestinal tract</a> of some animals, including humans. Giardia have a large sucking disk which permits them to adhere to the intestinal lining. The species that infects humans (and causes <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm">diarrhea</a>) is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/giardia_lamblia/article.htm">Giardia lamblia</a>. 

Named after the French biologist Alfred Mathieu Giard (1846-1908). See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23865">Giardia lamblia</a>.
