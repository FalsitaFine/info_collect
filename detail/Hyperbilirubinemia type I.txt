
Hyperbilirubinemia type I: Better known as Gilbert's disease, a common but harmless genetic condition in which a liver enzyme essential to the disposal of bilirubin (the chemical that results from the normal breakdown of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hemoglobin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">hemoglobin</a> from red blood cells) is abnormal. (The errant enzyme is called UDP-
glucuronosyltransferase).
