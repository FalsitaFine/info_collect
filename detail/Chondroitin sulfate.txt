
Chondroitin sulfate: A glycosaminoglycan (formerly called a mucopolysaccharide) found in cartilage, bone, blood vessels and connective tissues. There are two forms: chondroitin sulfate A and chondroitin sulfate C. One or both types accumulate abnormally in several of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11679" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mucopolysaccharidosis</a> disorders. Chondroitin sulfate B is now called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24046" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">dermatan sulfate</a>.
