
Gastric MALT lymphoma: A low-grade malignant lymphoma of the stomach associated with chronic infection by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2416" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bacterium</a> H. pylori. Three-quarters of gastric MALT lymphomas regress after the eradication of H. pylori with antibiotics. The one-quarter that are unresponsive to antibiotics carry a characteristic chromosomal translocation or are at an advanced stage. The translocation is of the t(11;18)(q21;q21) type.
