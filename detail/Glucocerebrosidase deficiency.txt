
Glucocerebrosidase deficiency: Causes type 1 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/gaucher_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Gaucher disease</a>, a progressive <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/genetic_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">genetic disease</a> due to an enzyme defect. The enzyme, glucocerebrosidase, is needed to break down the chemical glucocerebroside. The enzyme defect leads to the progressive accumulation of glucocerebroside in the spleen, liver, and lymph nodes. Gaucher disease is the most common genetic disease in Ashkenazi Jews (those of European origin) and in American and Canadian of Jewish descent.
