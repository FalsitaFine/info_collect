
IUD (intrauterine contraceptive device): A device  inserted into the uterus (womb) to prevent conception (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a>).   The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/iud/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">IUD</a> can be a coil, loop, triangle, or T in shape made of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/plastic/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">plastic</a>   or metal.  An IUD is inserted into the uterus by a health-care professional.
