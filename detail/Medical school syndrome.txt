
Medical school syndrome: A form of acute <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18718" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hypochondriasis</a> that affects most people in training to be a physician.  For example, when studying Hodgkin disease, a medical student feels behind their ears or neck, feels little lymph nodes (that are entirely normal), and thinks they have Hodgkin disease.
