
Effusion, pericardial: Too much fluid within the fibrous sac (pericardium) that surrounds the heart. The inner surface of the pericardium is lined by a layer of flat cells (mesothelial cells) that normally secrete a small amount of fluid, which acts as a lubricant to allow normal heart movement within the chest. A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4832" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pericardial effusion</a> involves the presence of an excessive amount of pericardial fluid, a pale yellow serous fluid, within the pericardium.
