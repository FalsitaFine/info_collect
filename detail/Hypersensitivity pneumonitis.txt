
Hypersensitivity pneumonitis: a type of inflammation of the lung that develops from breathing in a foreign substance. Molds (fungi) or dust are the most common substances that cause this reaction. Symptoms vary according to severity and can include <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/shortness_of_breath/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="SYM">shortness of breath</a>, coughing, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="DT">fever</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chills/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="SYM">chills</a>. The symptoms may develop within 4-6 hours after inhaling the offending substance, or, the symptoms may be chronic in cases in which there is continuous exposure to the agent.
