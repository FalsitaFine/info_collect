
Femara: Brand name for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/letrozole/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">letrozole</a>, an oral antiestrogen. Femara inhibits the enzyme aromatase in the  adrenal glands that produces the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estrogens_conjugated-vaginal_cream/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">estrogens</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estradiol/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">estradiol</a> and estrone) and thereby lowers their levels.  It works by decreasing the amount of estrogen the body makes. This can slow or stop the growth of many types of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_facts_stages/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast cancer</a> cells that need estrogen to grow.  See also: Aromatase inhibitor; monograph on Femara (letrozole).
