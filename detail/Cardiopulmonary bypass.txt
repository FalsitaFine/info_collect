
Cardiopulmonary bypass: Bypass of the heart and lungs (for example, during open-heart surgery). Blood returning to the heart is diverted through a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6414" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">heart-lung machine</a> (a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/urinary_tract_infection/article.htm2" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pump-oxygenator</a>) before it is returned to the arterial <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2735" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">circulation</a>. The machine does the work of both the heart and the lungs, by pumping blood as well as supplying oxygen to red blood cells.
