
Elbow, cellulitis of the: Inflammation of the skin
around the elbow due to infection (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cellulitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cellulitis</a>) commonly occurs as a result of abrasions or
puncture wounds permitting bacteria on the surface of the skin to invade the deeper layers
of the skin. This causes inflamed skin characterized by heat, redness, warmth, and
swelling. The most common bacteria that cause cellulitis include Staphylococcus
("Staph") and Streptococcus ("Streop"). One can have an associated
low-grade <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a>. Cellulitis generally requires antibiotic treatment, either orally or
intravenously. Heat application can help in the healing process.
