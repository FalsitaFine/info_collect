
Lentigo: A type of freckle that is a small tan, brown, or black spot which tends to be darker than the usual (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31807" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ephelis</a>-type) freckle and which do not fade in the winter. This kind of spot is referred to as lentigo simplex. Although lentigines may be part of a genetic disorder, for the most part they are just isolated and unimportant spots. (The genetic disorder in which lentigines occur is called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31833" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">LEOPARD syndrome</a>.)  Lentigo is the Latin word for lentil. A lentigo looks like a lentil bean. The plural is lentigines.
