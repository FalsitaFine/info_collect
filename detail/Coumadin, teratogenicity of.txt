
Coumadin, teratogenicity of: The anticoagulant ("blood-
thinner") Coumadin is a known teratogen, an agent that can disturb the development of the embryo and fetus and lead to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_defects/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">birth defects</a>..  Coumadin taken by a woman during <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a> can cause bleeding into the baby's brain (cerebral hemorrhage), underdevelopment  (hypoplasia) of the baby's nose and stippling of the ends (the epiphyses) of the baby's long bones.
Coumadin is a brand name of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/warfarin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">warfarin</a>.
