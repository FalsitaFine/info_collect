
HBV: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3708">Hepatitis B</a> virus. A virus which primarily causes <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3979">inflammation</a> of the liver. The hepatitis B virus can be transmitted in several ways including <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2491">blood transfusion</a>, needle sticks, body <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11872">piercing</a> and tattooing using unsterile instruments, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2980">dialysis</a>, sexual and even less intimate close contact, and childbirth. Symptoms include fatigue, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/jaundice_in_adults/article.htm">jaundice</a>, nausea, vomiting, dark urine, and light stools. Diagnosis is by blood test(s).
