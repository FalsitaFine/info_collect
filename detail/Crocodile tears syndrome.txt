
Crocodile tears syndrome: Spontaneous tearing in parallel with the normal salivation of eating. The crocodile tears syndrome occurs most often following facial paralysis when nerve fibers destined for a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=14387" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">salivary gland</a> are damaged and by mistake regrow into a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/angina_symptoms/article.htm00" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tear gland</a>. Also called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/angina_symptoms/article.htm01" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Bogorad's syndrome</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/angina_symptoms/article.htm02" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">gustatolacrimal reflex</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/angina_symptoms/article.htm03" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">paroxysmal lacrimation</a>.
