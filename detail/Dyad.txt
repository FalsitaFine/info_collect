
Dyad: The word "dyad" comes from the Greek "dyas" meaning the number two. In psychology, a dyad refers to a pair of persons in an interactional situation. For example, a patient and therapist, a woman and her husband, a girl and her stepfather, etc.
In chemistry, a dyad is a bivalent element. And in biology, a dyad is a double chromosome resulting from the splitting of a tetrad (a quadruple chromosome) during <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4335" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">meiosis</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8591" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">germ cell</a> formation).
