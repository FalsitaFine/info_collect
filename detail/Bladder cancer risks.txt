
Bladder cancer risks: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/smoking_and_quitting_smoking/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Smoking</a> is a major risk factor.
Cigarette smokers develop <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/bladder_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">bladder cancer</a> 2-3 times more often than do nonsmokers. Quitting
smoking reduces the risk of bladder <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/lung_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">lung cancer</a>, several other types of cancer,
and a number of other diseases as well. Workers in some occupations are at higher risk of
developing bladder cancer because of exposure to carcinogens (cancer-causing substances)
in the workplace. These workers include people in the rubber, chemical, and leather
industries, as well as hairstylists, machinists, metal workers, printers, painters,
textile workers, and truck drivers.
