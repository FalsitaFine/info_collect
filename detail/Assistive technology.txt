
Assistive technology: This term may be used to 
mean an assistive device, but more commonly denotes some 
kind of electronic or computerized device that helps a 
disabled person to function more easily in the world. 
Examples of assistive technology include devices that allow 
people to control a computer with the mouth, keyboards that 
can "speak" for mute individuals, and closed captioning 
systems that help the hearing impaired enjoy television 
shows and videos.
