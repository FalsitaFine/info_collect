
In situ hybridization: The use of a DNA or RNA <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5052" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">probe</a> to detect complementary genetic material in cells or tissue. In situ hybridization involves hybridizing a labeled <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4594" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">nucleic acid</a> to suitably prepared cells or tissues on microscope slides to allow visualization in situ (in the normal location).
