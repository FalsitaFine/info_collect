
Isoform: A protein that has the same function as another protein but which is encoded by a different gene and may have small differences in its sequence. For example, transforming factor beta (TGF-B) exists in three versions, or isoforms (TGF-B1, TGF-B2, and TGF-B3), each of which can set off a signaling <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32065" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cascade</a> that starts in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2907" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cytoplasm</a> and terminates in the nucleus of the cell.
