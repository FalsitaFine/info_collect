
Citrulline: A non-standard amino acid that is not normally present in protein. Citrulline is created in the body as an intermediate in the conversion of the amino acid ornithine to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7006" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">arginine</a> in a metabolic pathway called the urea cycle. Citrulline was first isolated from watermelon. The term citrulline was coined in 1930 from citrullus, the Latin name of the watermelon.
