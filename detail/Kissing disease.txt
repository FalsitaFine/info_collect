
Kissing disease: A name for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/infectious_mononucleosis/article.htm">infectious mononucleosis</a> 
("mono"), a very common illness caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV). By the
time most people reach adulthood, an antibody against EBV can be detected in their blood
meaning they have been infected with EBV. The illness is less severe in young children.
The infection can be spread by saliva. The incubation period for "mono" is 4 to
8 weeks. Symptoms include <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm">fever</a>, fatigue, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sore_throat_pharyngitis/article.htm">sore throat</a>, and swollen lymph glands.
"Mono" can cause liver inflammation (hepatitis) and spleen enlargement. Vigorous
contact sports should be avoided to prevent spleen rupture.
