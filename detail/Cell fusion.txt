
Cell fusion: The melding of two or more cells into one cell called a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/gout_gouty_arthritis/article.htm2" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">heterokaryon</a>. A heterokaryon may reproduce itself for at least several generations. Cell fusion provides a method for assigning specific genes to specific chromosomes. When an undifferentiated stem cell fuses with a mature differentiated cell, the resultant cell can retain the mature cell phenotype.
