
Inkblot test: A test used in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15156" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">clinical psychology</a> and psychiatry involving inkblots. The inkblots are used to determine what a person perceives (reads into) in the enigmatic and highly ambiguous shapes. Ten standardized blots are shown one at a time to a subject and their responses are recorded. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15154" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Rorschach test</a> became a common psychological test and is still in use in psychology and psychiatry. The test was created by Dr. Hermann Rorschach (1884-1922), a young Swiss psychiatrist. He published the test (also called the Rorschach test) in 1921, not long before his untimely death.
