
Bx: Abbreviation for biopsy, the removal of a sample of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5800">tissue</a> for examination or other study. 
Biopsies are most frequently studied by use of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4375">microscope</a> to check for possible abnormalities such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3979">inflammation</a> or cancer. A biopsy may also be used for specialized purposes such as to start cell cultures to analyze a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3573">genetic</a> disease or the process of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13403">aging</a> in the laboratory.
BX: Medical abbreviation for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2466">biopsy</a>. See: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9959">bx</a>.
