
Breast surgeon: Usually a general surgeon who is especially skilled in operating on the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast</a>. A breast surgeon may biopsy a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a> in the breast and, if it is malignant, remove the tumor. A breast surgeon may also do <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_reconstruction/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">breast reconstruction</a> following a mastectomy for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_facts_stages/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast cancer</a>. The surgeons who carry out this type of breast reconstruction may be breast oncology surgeons or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/plastic/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">plastic</a> surgeons. (Surgeons trained in both these specialities are known as oncoplastic surgeons.)  Some breast surgeons also do cosmetic breast surgery.
