
Gene, Y-linked: A gene on the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6049" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Y chromosome</a>.   (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15729" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Y-linkage</a> is analogous to X-linkage (the presence of a gene on the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6030" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">X chromosome</a>) in that it says a gene is on one of the sex chromosomes.)  It has often been said that little is known about genes that may be <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6059" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Y-linked</a>. This is no longer true.  As of the year 2012, about three dozen genes were known to be Y-linked including:  <ul> <li>ASMTY  (which stands for acetyl serotonin methyltransferase),   <li>TSPY  (testis-specific protein),   <li>IL3RAY (interleukin-3 receptor),   <li>SRY (sex-determining region),   <li>TDF (testis determining factor),   <li>ZFY  (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25292" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">zinc finger</a> protein), PRKY  (protein kinase, Y-linked),   <li>AMGL  (amelogenin),   <li>CSF2RY  (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8780" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">granulocyte</a>-macrophage <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2793" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">colony-stimulating factor</a> receptor, alpha subunit on the Y chromosome),   <li>ANT3Y (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2143" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">adenine</a> nucleotide translocator-3 on the Y),   <li>AZF2  (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15755" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">azoospermia</a> factor 2),   <li>BPY2  (basic protein on the Y chromosome),   <li>AZF1 (azoospermia factor 1),   <li>DAZ (deleted in azoospermia),   <li>RBM1 (RNA binding motif protein, Y chromosome, family 1, member A1),  <li>RBM2 (RNA binding motif protein 2) and   <li>UTY  (ubiquitously transcribed TPR gene on Y chromosome).</li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></ul>
