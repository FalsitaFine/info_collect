
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/measles_rubeola/article.htm">Measles</a>: An acute and highly contagious viral 
disease characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm">fever</a>, runny nose, cough, red eyes, and a 
spreading skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm">rash</a>. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/measles_rubeola/article.htm">Measles</a>, also known as rubeola, is a potentially 
disastrous disease. It can be complicated by ear infections, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm"> pneumonia</a>, encephalitis (which can cause convulsions, mental 
retardation, and even death), the sudden onset of low blood platelet 
levels with severe bleeding (acute thrombocytopenic purpura), or a 
chronic brain disease that occurs months to years after an attack of 
measles (subacute sclerosing panencephalitis). During pregnancy, 
exposure to the measles virus may trigger <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/miscarriage_causes_and_symptoms/article.htm">miscarriage</a> or premature 
delivery. Treatment includes rest, calamine lotion or other anti-
itching preparations to soothe the skin, non-aspirin pain relievers 
for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm">fever</a>, and in some cases antibiotics. Measles can often be 
prevented through vaccination. Also known as hard measles, seven-day 
measles, eight-day measles, nine-day measles, ten-day measles, 
morbilli. See also measles encephalitis; measles immunization; 
measles syndrome, atypical; MMR.
