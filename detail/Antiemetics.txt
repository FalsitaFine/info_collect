
Antiemetics: 1. As a noun, a drug taken to prevent or treat <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/nausea_and_vomiting/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">nausea and vomiting</a>. As, for example, the antihistamine <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/meclizine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">meclizine</a> hydrochloride (Bonine).<br>
2. As an adjective, pertaining to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prevention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">prevention</a> or treatment of nausea and vomiting. Opening a window in a car can have an antiemetic effect.<br>
From anti- + emetic.</br></br>
