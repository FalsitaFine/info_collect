
Disorder, attention deficit (ADD): Often referred to as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">attention deficit hyperactivity disorder</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ADD</a> or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ADHD</a>), a disorder that is characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hyperactivity/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">hyperactivity</a> or restlessness, trouble concentrating, and/or trouble controlling one's impulses. It is one of the most common conditions of childhood and adolescence. Most experts agree that this condition affects 8%-10% of school-age children and that more than 40% of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">children with ADHD</a> grow up into adults with this disorder. Rather than having any single cause, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_children_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">attention deficit hyperactivity disorder</a>(<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_children_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">ADHD</a>) develops from both genetic and life experience risk factors. Individuals with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">ADHD</a> tend to have problems developing efficient self-regulation skills and decision-making abilities. Biologically, ADHD is a neurochemical and neuroanatomical disorder.
