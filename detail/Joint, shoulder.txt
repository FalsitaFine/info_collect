
Joint, shoulder: The shoulder has two main bones: the scapula (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8207" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">shoulder blade</a>) and the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3814" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">humerus</a> (the long bone of the upper arm). The end of the scapula, called the glenoid, is a socket into which the head of the humerus fits to form a flexible <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8748" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ball-and-socket joint</a>.
