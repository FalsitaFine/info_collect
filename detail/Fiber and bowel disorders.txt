
Fiber and bowel disorders: High <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fiber/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">fiber</a> diets help delay  the progression of diverticulosis and, at least, reduce the bouts of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diverticulosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diverticulitis</a>. In  many cases, it helps reduce the symptoms of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/irritable_bowel_syndrome_ibs_triggers_prevention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Irritable Bowel Syndrome</a> (IBS) (also  called spastic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colitis</a>, mucus colitis, and nervous colon syndrome.) It is generally  accepted that a diet high in fiber is protective, or at least reduces the incidence, of   <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colon_polyps/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colon polyps</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colon_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colon cancer</a>.
