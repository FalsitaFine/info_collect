
Macroorchidism: Abnormally large testes. To determine if the testes are too large, a device called an orchidometer is used that permits a testis to be compared to a series of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/plastic/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">plastic</a> ovals (like miniature American footballs) of differing sizes. Macroorchidism is a diagnostic feature, for example, of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fragile_x_syndrome/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fragile X syndrome</a>, the most common inherited form of mental retardation. The opposite of macroorchidism is microorchidism.
