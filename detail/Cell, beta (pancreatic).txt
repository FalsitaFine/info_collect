
Cell, beta (pancreatic): A type of cell in the pancreas (the organ of the
digestive system located behind the stomach). Within the pancreas, the beta
cells are located in areas called the islets of Langerhans. They constitute
the predominant type of cell in the islets. The beta cells are important
because they make <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/insulin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">insulin</a>. Degeneration of the beta cells is the main cause
of type I (insulin-dependent) <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a> mellitus.
