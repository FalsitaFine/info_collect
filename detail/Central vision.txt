
Central vision: A process in which millions of cells change light into nerve signals that tell the brain what the person is seeing. As a person reads, drives, and performs other activities that require fine, sharp, straight-ahead vision, light is focused onto the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4241" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">macula</a> in the center of the retina, the light-sensitive layer of tissue at the back of the eye.
