
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4001" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Intervening sequence</a>: Part of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3560" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">gene</a> that is initially transcribed from the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13963" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">DNA</a> into the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12237" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">primary</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5382" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">RNA</a> transcript but then is excised (removed) from it when the so-called exons sequences on either side of it are spliced together.
