
Diplopia, monocular: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/double_vision/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Double vision</a> (diplopia) in only one eye. The double vision persists when the other eye is covered. Monocular diplopia can be caused by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/astigmatism_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">astigmatism</a> (abnormal curvature of the front of the cornea), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/keratoconus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">keratoconus</a> (cone-shaped cornea), pterygium (thickening of the conjunctiva over the cornea), a cataract, dislocation of the lens, a mass in the eyelid pressing on the front of the eye, xerophthalmia (dry eye), and certain retinal problems. Treatment depends upon the specific cause.
