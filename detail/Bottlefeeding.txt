
Bottlefeeding: The practice of feeding an infant a substitute for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast</a> milk. Pediatricians generally advise exclusively breastfeeding (that is, breastfeeding with no supplementary formula) for all full-term, healthy infants for the first 6 months of life. However, many infants are bottlefed today, at least in part. For infants to achieve normal growth and maintain normal health, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/infant_formulas/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">infant formulas</a> must include proper amounts of water, carbohydrate, protein, fat, vitamins, and minerals.
