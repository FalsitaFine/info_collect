
Hypergraphia: The driving compulsion to write; the overwhelming urge to write. Hypergraphia may compel someone to keep a voluminous journal, to jot off frequent letters to the editor, to write on toilet paper if nothing else is available, and perhaps even to compile a dictionary. Hypergraphia is the opposite of writer's block. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=26725" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Temporal lobe epilepsy</a> is associated with hypergraphia. This association has been known at least as early as 1974 (Waxman SG, Geschwind N. Hypergraphia in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20829" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">temporal lobe</a> epilepsy. Neurology. 1974;24:629-36). A number of prolific writer may have had temporal lobe epilepsy, including Byron, Dante, Dostoevsky, Moliere, Petrarch, Poe, and Tennyson.
Hypergraphia has also been called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=26494" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">midnight disease</a>.
