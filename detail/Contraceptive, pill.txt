
Contraceptive, pill: Commonly called "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/oral_contraceptives_birth_control_pills/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">the pill</a>," combined <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/oral_contraceptives_birth_control_pills/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">oral contraceptives</a> are the most commonly used form of reversible <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_control_methods/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">birth control</a> in the United States. This form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_control_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">birth control</a> suppresses <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ovulation_and_fertility_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">ovulation</a> (the monthly release of an egg from the ovaries) by the combined actions of the hormones estrogen and progestin. If a woman remembers to take <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_control_pill_vs_depo-provera_shot/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">the pill</a> every day as directed, she has an extremely low chance of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/trying_to_conceive/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">becoming pregnant</a> in a year. But the pill's effectiveness may be reduced if the woman is taking some medications, such as certain antibiotics.
