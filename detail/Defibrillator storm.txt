
Defibrillator storm: A condition that arises when a defibrillator implanted in the chest to correct an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/arrhythmia_irregular_heartbeat/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">arrhythmia</a> (an abnormal heart rhythm) fires off frequently --sometimes several times a day or even more often -- each time with a jolt like a boxer's punch to the chest, because of far advanced <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heart_disease_coronary_artery_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">heart disease</a>. Defibrillator storm is an uncommon but grave complication with an implantable defibrillator (a device that can profitably prolong a patient's life but can also make a patient's life painful.)
