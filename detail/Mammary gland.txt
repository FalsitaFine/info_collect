
Mammary gland: One of the two half-moon-shaped glands 
on either side of the adult female chest, which with fatty tissue and 
the nipple make up the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm">breast</a>. Within each mammary gland is a network 
of sacs that produce milk during lactation and send it to the nipple 
via a system of ducts. Undeveloped mammary glands are present in 
female children and in males. See also <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_anatomy/article.htm">Breast</a>, Lactation.
