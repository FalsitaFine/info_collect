
Garlic: A perennial herb of the lily family cultivated for its pungent, edible bulbs. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/garlic_allium_sativum_l-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">Garlic</a> has been used with the intention of lowering blood pressure and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cholesterol_management/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cholesterol</a>.  Preliminary studies suggest that garlic consumption may reduce the risk of developing several types of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a>, especially cancers of the gastrointestinal tract.    Side effects include breath and body odor, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heartburn_reflux/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">heartburn</a>, upset stomach, and allergic reactions.  These effects are more common with raw garlic. Garlic can decrease normal blood clotting in a manner similar to aspirin.  Caution should be used if surgery or dental work is planned and in patients taking medications to prevent blood clotting (anticoagulants) such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/warfarin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">warfarin</a> /Coumadin. Garlic has been found to interfere with the effectiveness of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/saquinavir/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">saquinavir</a>, a drug used to treat HIV infection.  Effects on other drugs has not been well studied.
