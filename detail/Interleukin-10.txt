
Interleukin-10: Abbreviated IL-10. An antiinflammatory and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25652" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immunosuppressive</a> substance produced within the body. IL-10 plays a role in the regulation of immune responses. It is secreted by antigen-presenting cells, promotes the development of immunologic tolerance, and suppresses the production of inflammatory cytokines. The gene for IL-10 is in chromosome region 1q31-q32. 
IL-10 modulates acute <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3628" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">graft-versus-host disease</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11391" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">GVHD</a>), a major complication in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31110" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bone marrow transplant</a>. The presence of a particular variant in the recipient's IL-10 gene is associated with a substantial decrease in the risk of GVHD. Knowledge of the recipient's IL-10 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8472" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genotype</a> helps predict the chance of GVHD.
