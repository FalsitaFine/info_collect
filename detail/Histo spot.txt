
Histo spot: A small circular punched-out spot in the retina at a site where spores of the fungus histoplasma (histo) seeded in the eye. Histo spots may be multiple, found in both eyes, and scattered throughout the retina.  Histo spots in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4241" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">macula</a> are significant since they may stimulate the growth of abnormal new blood vessels located under the retina. These new blood vessels may suddenly bleed, causing permanent macular scarring and impairing <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8544" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">central vision</a>. If the process is detected at an early stage, it may be successfully treated with focal retinal laser.
