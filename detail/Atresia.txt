
Atresia: Absence of a normal opening, or failure of a structure to be tubular. Atresia can affect many structures in the body. For example, esophageal atresia is a birth defect in which part of the esophagus is not hollow, and with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=16239" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anal atresia</a>, there is no hole at the bottom end of the intestine.
