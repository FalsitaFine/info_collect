
Designer estrogen: An engineered drug that possesses some, but not all, of the actions of estrogen. Also known as selective estrogen-receptor modulator (SERM). For example, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/raloxifene/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">raloxifene</a> (brand name: Evista) is classified as a designer estrogen because, like estrogen, it prevents bone loss and lowers serum <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cholesterol_management/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cholesterol</a>; however, it does not stimulate the endometrial lining of the uterus.
