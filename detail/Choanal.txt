
Choanal: Pertaining to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39034" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choana</a>, the passageway from the back of one side of the nose to the throat. The choana is shaped like the opening of a train tunnel in a mountain which then opens into the space called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm7" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">nasopharynx</a>. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39038" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choanae</a> must be open to permit breathing through the nose. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39036" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Choanal atresia</a> is blockage of the choanal passageway while <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39037" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choanal stenosis</a> is narrowing of that passageway. From the Greek choane (a funnel). The word choanal is pronounced co-anal but it has nothing to do with the anus.
