
Manometry: The measurement of pressure using a device called a manometer. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/esophageal_manometry/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">Esophageal manometry</a> is done to measure muscle pressure and movements in the esophagus in the evaluation of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/achalasia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">achalasia</a>. Anal manometry, the measurement of the pressure generated by the anal sphincter, is used to evaluate anal incompetence and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fecal_incontinence/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fecal incontinence</a>. A blood pressure cuff is a familiar form of sphygmomanometer.
