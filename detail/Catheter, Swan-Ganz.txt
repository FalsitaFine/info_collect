
Catheter, Swan-Ganz: A style of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/oximetry/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">oximetry</a> catheter that is inserted into a major vein under the collarbone or in the neck, threaded through the right side of the heart, and then threaded into the pulmonary artery. Physicians can use monitoring equipment with a Swan-Ganz catheter to measure blood pressure inside the heart and to find out how much blood the heart is pumping.
