
Char syndrome: A genetic disorder characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4797" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">patent</a> ductus arteriosis and unusual facial features including a long <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/strep_throat_gas/article.htm1" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">philtrum</a> (increase in the distance between the nose and upper lip), down-slanting palpebral fissures (eye slits), and thick lips as well as incurving fifth fingers. The syndrome is inherited as an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11974" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">autosomal dominant</a> trait. The gene responsible for it is in chromosome 6p12. The gene is called TFAP2B. It is a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25194" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">transcription factor</a> expressed in neuroectoderm during embryonic development. The disorder was first described in 1978 by F. Char  as "peculiar <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3370" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">facies</a> with short philtrum, duck-bill lips, ptosis, and low-set ears."
