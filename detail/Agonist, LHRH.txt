
Agonist, LHRH: A compound that is similar to LHRH (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4210" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone</a>) in structure and is able to it performs its action(s). Luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone is a naturally occurring hormone that controls sex hormones in both men and women. Thus, an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4153" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">LHRH agonist</a> serves in a manner similar to LHRH to control the same sex hormones. 
An <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7835" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">agonist</a> achieves a normal action, often mimicking the action of a naturally occurring substance. The word "agonist" comes from the Late Latin <i>agnista</i>, contender, from the Greek <i>agnists</i>, contestant, from <i>agn</i>, contest. An agonist is a chemical contestant.
