
Lindane: An organochlorine pesticide and nerve poison. Also a suspected carcinogen (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a>-causing agent). Although many countries have banned lindane, it is still used in the US for treating <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/head_lice/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">head lice</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/scabies/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">scabies</a>. Also known as gamma-hexachlorocyclohexane (HCH).
The US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) in 2003 announced stronger warnings and a medication guide to be distributed directly to patients purchasing Lindane Lotion and Lindane Shampoo for the treatment of scabies and lice.
