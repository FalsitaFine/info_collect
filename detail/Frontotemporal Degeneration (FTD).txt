

Frontotemporal degeneration (FTD): Degeneration of the cells in the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain causes this group of brain disorders. It is also referred to as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/image-collection/frontotemporal_dementia_ftd_picture/picture.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="img">frontotemporal dementia</a>, frontotemporal lobar degeneration (FTLD), or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pick_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Pick's disease</a>. FTD typically occurs in younger people than <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/alzheimers_disease_causes_stages_and_symptoms/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Alzheimer's disease</a>, with most cases occurring between the ages of 45 and 64.  It can develop in ages ranging from the 20s to the 80s. Symptoms include a gradual decline in function related to behavior, language, or movement. Memory is usually relatively preserved in people with FTD.
