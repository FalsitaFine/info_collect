
Breast implants: prosthetic devices surgically inserted in order to change the size, shape, and/or contour of a person's breast. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_augmentation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">Breast implants</a> may be used as part of a cosmetic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_augmentation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">breast augmentation</a> procedure or may be placed during post-<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">mastectomy</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_reconstruction/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">breast reconstruction</a>. Less commonly, they are used to correct deformities or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_defects/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">birth defects</a> of the chest.  Breast implants may be made of different materials, including saline solution, silicone gel, or a composite filler.
