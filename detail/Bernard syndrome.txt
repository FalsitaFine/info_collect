
Bernard syndrome: A complex of abnormal findings,
namely sinking in of one eyeball, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4041" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ipsilateral</a> ptosis (drooping of the upper eyelid on the
same side) and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4393" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">miosis</a> (constriction of the pupil of that eye) together with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2261" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anhidrosis </a>
(lack of sweating) and flushing of the affected side of the face. Due to paralysis of
certain nerves (specifically, the cervical sympathetic nerves). Also called Horner-Bernard
syndrome, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11973" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Bernard-Horner syndrome</a> and Horner's ptosis, but best known today as Horner
syndrome.
