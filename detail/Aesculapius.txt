
Aesculapius: The ancient Roman god of medicine, whose staff with a snake curled around it is commonly used as a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39915" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">symbol of medicine</a>. According to mythology, Aesculapius's children included Hygeia, the goddess of health, and Panaceia, the goddess of healing.
