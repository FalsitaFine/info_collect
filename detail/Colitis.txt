
Colitis: Inflammation of the large intestine (the  colon). There are many forms of  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colitis</a>, including <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ulcerative_colitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ulcerative colitis</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/crohns_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Crohn's disease</a>, infectious,  pseudomembranous, and spastic. For example, intermittent <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blood_in_the_stool_rectal_bleeding/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rectal bleeding</a>, crampy abdominal  pain and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a> can be symptoms of ulcerative colitis. Diagnosis can be made by direct visualization using (sigmoidoscopy or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colonoscopy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">colonoscopy</a>) which is the most accurate test.  Long-standing ulcerative colitis increases the risk for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colon_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colon cancer</a>. Ulcerative colitis  can also be associated with inflammation in joints, spine, skin, eyes, the liver and its  bile ducts. Treatment of ulcerative colitis can involve medications and surgery.
