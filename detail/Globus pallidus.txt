
Globus pallidus: A comparatively pale-looking, spherical area in the brain. The globus pallidus is specifically part of the lentiform nucleus, which in turn is part of the striate body, a component of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10030" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">basal ganglia</a>. Also called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9035" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pale globe</a>, palladum, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9038" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">paleostriatum</a>.
