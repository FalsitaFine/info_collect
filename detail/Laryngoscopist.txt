
Laryngoscopist: A person who uses a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6221" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">laryngoscope</a>, a flexible, lighted tube used to   look at the inside of the larynx (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9019" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">voice box</a>). The laryngoscope is inserted through the mouth into the upper airway.     <u>History:</u> The laryngoscope was invented in 1830 by Benjamin Guy Babington, a British physician who also first described the disease now called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6678" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia</a> and who made considerable contributions to epidemiology.  <u>Etymology:</u> The word "laryngoscope" was compounded from "laryngo-" + the Greek "skopeo," to inspect = to inspect the larynx.
