
Mutation, hereditary: A heritable change in the DNA of a gene or chromosome in a cell destined to become an egg or a sperm or the zygote (the conceptus) at the single-cell stage. When transmitted to a child, an hereditary mutation is incorporated in every cell of their body. Hereditary mutations play a key role in genetic diseases. They also play a role in certain types of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a> as, for example, the eye <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/retinoblastoma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">retinoblastoma</a> and Wilms tumor, a childhood malignancy of the kidney.
Hereditary mutations are also called germline mutations.
