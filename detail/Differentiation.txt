
Differentiation: 1  The process by which cells become progressively more specialized; a normal process through which cells mature. This process of specialization for the cell comes at the expense of its breadth of potential. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stem_cells/article.htm">Stem cells</a> can, for example, differentiate into secretory cells in the intestine. 
2 In <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm">cancer</a>, differentiation refers to how mature (developed) the cancer cells are in a tumor. Differentiated tumor cells resemble normal cells and tend to grow and spread at a slower rate than undifferentiated or poorly differentiated tumor cells, which lack the structure and function of normal cells and grow uncontrollably.
