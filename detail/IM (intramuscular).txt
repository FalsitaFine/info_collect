
IM (intramuscular): An IM medication is given by   needle into the muscle. This is as opposed to a medication that is   given by a needle into the skin (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4009" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">intradermal</a>) , just   below the skin (subcutaneous) or into a vein (intravenous).
