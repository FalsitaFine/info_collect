
Choking (object in airway): Partial or complete obstruction of the airway can be due to a foreign body (e.g., food, a bead, toy, etc.)  The onset of respiratory distress may be sudden with cough. There is often agitation in the early stage of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8541" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">airway obstruction</a>. The signs of respiratory distress include labored, ineffective breathing until the person is not longer breathing (apneic). Loss of consciousness occurs if the obstruction is not relieved.
