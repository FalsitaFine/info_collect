
Myocarditis, viral: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3979">Inflammation</a> of the muscles in the walls of the heart due to a viral infection. Among the offenders are dozen of different common viruses, including the 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8698">adenovirus</a>, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22971">coxsackievirus</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4972">poliovirus</a>, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3183">echoviruses</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3980">influenza</a> viruses. Destruction of the heart muscle cells may lead to dilated <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13590">cardiomyopathy</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3672">heart failure</a>. See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/polymyalgia_rheumatica/article.htm6">Myocarditis</a>.
