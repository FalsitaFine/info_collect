
Lateral meniscus of the knee: A thickened crescent-shaped cartilage pad in the outer portion of the joint formed by the femur (thigh bone) and the tibia (shin bone). The lateral meniscus acts as a smooth surface for the joint to move on. The lateral meniscus is toward the outer side of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8845" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">knee joint</a>. It serves to evenly load the surface during weight-bearing, and also aids in disbursing joint fluid for joint lubrication. See also <i>knee.</i>
