
Fibrocystic breast condition: a common condition characterized by lumpiness in one or both breasts due to the presence of multiple small <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cyst/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cysts</a> and masses. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fibrocystic_breast_condition/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Fibrocystic breast condition</a> is benign and is not a type of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_facts_stages/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast cancer</a>. The condition affects more than 60% of women and is most commonly found in women between the ages of 30 and 50. Some women with the condition may experience symptoms like breast tenderness and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_pain/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">breast pain</a>. The development of fibrocystic breast condition is believed to be related to the normal hormonal variation during the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/menstruation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">menstrual cycle</a>. The lumps found in the breasts can sometimes mimic the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_symptoms_and_signs/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">symptoms of breast cancer</a>, or they may mask the symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancers</a>.
