
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4044" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Iris</a>, speckled:  Due to little white (or lightly colored) spots that are slightly elevated on the surface of the iris. These spots, arranged <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> a ring concentric with the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/urinary_tract_infection/article.htm5" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pupil</a>, occur normally, but more frequently, in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3112" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Down syndrome</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=38317" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">trisomy</a> 21). They were described in 1924 by Thomas Brushfield, are called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6570" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Brushfield's spots</a>, and are due to aggregation of a normal iris element (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2824" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">connective tissue</a>).
