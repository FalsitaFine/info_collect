
Newborn screening: Tests of newborns to screen for serious treatable diseases most of which are genetic. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/babies_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">newborn</a> screening tests done in the United States are decided on a state-by-state basis. 
<ul><li>The most common newborn screening tests in the US include those for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypothyroidism/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hypothyroidism</a> (underactivity of the thyroid gland), PKU (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/phenylketonuria/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">phenylketonuria</a>), galactosemia, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sickle_cell/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sickle cell disease</a>.</li><li> Testing for hypothyroidism and PKU is required in virtually all States. </li><li>Screening for galactosemia and sickle cell disease is required in most states. </li><li>Some states in the US mandate tests for other conditions. These include: maple syrup urine disease (MSUD), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/homocysteine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">homocystinuria</a>, biotinidase deficiency, congenital adrenal hyperplasia, MCAD, tyrosinemia, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cystic_fibrosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cystic fibrosis</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/toxoplasmosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">toxoplasmosis</a>.</li><li> All these tests are usually done using the same sample of the baby's blood.</li></ul>
