
MCH:  Abbreviation for mean cell <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hemoglobin/article.htm">hemoglobin</a>, which is the average amount of hemoglobin in the average red cell. The MCH is a calculated value derived from the measurement of hemoglobin and the red cell count. (The hemoglobin value is the amount of hemoglobin in a volume of blood while the red cell count is the number of red blood cells in a volume of blood.) It is a standard part of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/complete_blood_count/article.htm">complete blood count</a>.
