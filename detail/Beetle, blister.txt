
Beetle, blister: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9523" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">blister beetle</a> is one of a number of beetles that have historically been used in dried or powdered form for medicinal purposes to raise blisters on the skin. An example of a blister beetle is the so-called Spanish fly. All blister beetles are soft-bodied beetles and belong to the family Meloidae.
