
Coenzyme: A substance that enhances the action of an enzyme. (An enzyme is a protein that functions as a catalyst to mediate and speed a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6760" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chemical reaction</a>). Coenzymes are small molecules. They cannot by themselves catalyze a reaction but they can help enzymes to do so. In technical terms, coenzymes are organic nonprotein molecules that bind with the protein molecule (apoenzyme) to form the active enzyme (holoenzyme).
