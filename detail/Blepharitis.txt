
Blepharitis: Inflammation of the eyelids. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blepharitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Blepharitis</a> occurs in two forms, anterior and posterior. Anterior blepharitis affects the outside front of the eyelid, where the eyelashes are attached. The two most common causes of anterior blepharitis are bacteria (Staphylococcus) and scalp <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/seborrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dandruff</a>. Posterior blepharitis affects the inner eyelid (the moist part that makes contact with the eye) and is caused by problems with the oil (meibomian) glands in this part of the eyelid. Two skin disorders can cause this form of blepharitis: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rosacea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rosacea</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/seborrheic_dermatitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">seborrheic dermatitis</a>.
