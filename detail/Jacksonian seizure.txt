
Jacksonian seizure: A form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/seizure/article.htm">epilepsy</a> involving brief alteration in movement, sensation or nerve 
function caused by abnormal electrical activity in a localized area of the 
brain. Jacksonian seizures is a form of simple complex seizures in which the 
abnormal electrical activity is localized to one region in the brain. Seizures 
of this type typically cause no change in awareness or alertness. They are 
transient, fleeting, and ephemeral.
