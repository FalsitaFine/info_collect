
Chin: Medically, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23435" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mentum</a>. The lower portion of the face below the lower lip including the prominence of the lower jaw and the line of fusion of the two separate halves of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31709" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">jawbone</a>  (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4269" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mandible</a>). This line of fusion (called the symphysis menti) encloses a triangular area at tip of the chin (termed the mental protuberance). On each side, below the second premolar tooth, is the mental foramen, an opening for the passage of blood vessels and a nerve that supply the chin.
