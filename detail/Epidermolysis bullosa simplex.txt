
Epidermolysis bullosa simplex: A variant of <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24545" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epidermolysis bullosa</a>.<a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24546" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Epidermolysis bullosa simplex</a> (<a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24551" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">EBS</a>) usually begins with blistering that is evident at birth or shortly afterward. <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">In</a> a localized, mild form of EBS called Weber-Cockayne, blisters rarely extend beyond the <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3405" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">feet</a> and hands. In some subtypes of EBS, the blisters occur over widespread areas of the body.
