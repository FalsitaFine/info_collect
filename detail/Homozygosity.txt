
Homozygosity: The state of possessing two identical forms of a particular gene, one inherited from each parent.   For example, a girl who is homozygous for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cystic_fibrosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cystic fibrosis</a> (CF) received the cystic fibrosis gene from both of her parents and therefore she has cystic fibrosis.   (A) heterozygote (possesses of two different forms of a particular gene, one inherited from each parent. For example, the father and mother of a cystic fibrosis child are heterozygotes for cystic fibrosis (CF). Each carries one normal gene and one cystic fibrosis gene but has no signs or symptoms of the disease.
