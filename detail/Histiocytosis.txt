
Histiocytosis: One of several disorders in which histiocytes start to multiply and proliferate abnormally. The result can be tissue damage, pain, the development of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a>-like lumps, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fatigue</a>, and other symptoms. If histiocytosis affects the pituitary gland, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a> insipidus may also develop. Treatment includes radiation and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chemotherapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">chemotherapy</a>, although for reasons unknown, some cases of histiocytosis go into remission without treatment.
