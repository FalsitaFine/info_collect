
Focal dystonia due to torticollis: Spasm of
the muscles in the neck that control the position of the head, causing the head to twist and turn to one side. In addition, the head may be pulled forward or
backward. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8251">Torticollis</a> is the most common form of focal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3152">dystonia</a>. For more about the dystonias, see: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dystonia/article.htm">Dystonia</a>.
