
Morphine: A powerful narcotic agent that has strong analgesic (pain relief) action and other significant effects on the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2667" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">central nervous system</a>. It is dangerously addicting. Morphine is a naturally occurring member of a large chemical class of compounds called alkaloids. The name, which derives from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33737" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Morpheus</a> (the mythologic god of dreams) was coined in 1805 by German apothecary Adolf Serturner to designate the main alkaloid in opium. Opium comes from the poppy plant.
