
Intrinsic: 1. An essential or inherent part of a something such as a structure.<br>
2. Coming from within, from the inside. Proteins have intrinsic signals that govern their transport and localization in the cell. <br>
From the Latin intrinsecus meaning situated on the inside. The opposite of intrinsic is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=40363" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">extrinsic</a>.</br></br>
