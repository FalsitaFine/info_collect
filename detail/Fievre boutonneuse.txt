
Fievre boutonneuse: African tick <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/typhus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">typhus</a>, one of the
tick-borne rickettsial diseases of the eastern hemisphere, similar to Rocky Mountain
spotted <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a>, but less severe, with fever, a small ulcer (tache noire) at the site of the
tick bite, swollen glands nearby (satellite lymphadenopathy), and a red raised
(maculopapular) <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rash</a>. The French word "boutonneuse" means pimply. Also called
boutonneuse and Conor and Bruch's disease.
