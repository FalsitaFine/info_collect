
Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS): A common gastrointestinal disorder involving an abnormal condition of gut contractions (motility) and increased gut sensations (visceral hypersensitivity)    characterized by   <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a> / discomfort , gas, bloating, mucous in stools, and irregular bowel   habits with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/constipation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">constipation</a> or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a> or alternating  diarrhea and constipation. Symptoms  tend to be chronic ,intermittent and to wax   and wane over the  years. Although IBS can cause chronic recurrent abdominal discomfort, it does not lead to any serious abdominal organ problems.   Making the diagnosis usually done by suspecting the condition and confirming irritable bowel by  excluding other causes of abdominal  illnesses. Treatment is directed toward relief of symptoms and includes changes in diet (eating a moderate high <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fiber/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">fiber</a> and avoiding <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/caffeine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">caffeine</a>, milk products and sweeteners), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">exercise</a>, relaxation techniques, and medications.  Also called spastic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colitis</a>, mucus colitis, nervous colon syndrome.
