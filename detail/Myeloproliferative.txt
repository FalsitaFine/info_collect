
Myeloproliferative: Referring to the proliferation of the bone marrow cells that give rise to red blood cells, granulocytes (a type of white blood cells), and/or platelets (crucial to blood clotting). The term myeloproliferative is in contrast to lymphoproliferative which refers to proliferation of lymphoid cells and reticuloendothelial cells such as macrophages. Both terms -- myeloproliferative and lymphoproliferative -- can be applied to conditions that are entirely benign, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12443" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">premalignant</a>, or frankly malignant.
