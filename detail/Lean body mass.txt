
Lean body mass: The mass of the body minus the fat (storage lipid). There are a number of methods for determining the lean body mass. Some of these methods require specialized equipment such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25888" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">underwater weighing</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25889" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hydrostatic weighing</a>), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25892" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">BOD POD</a> (a computerized chamber),  and DEXA (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry). Other methods for determining the lean body mass are simple such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25893" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">skin calipers</a> and  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25895" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bioelectric impedance analysis</a> (BIA).
