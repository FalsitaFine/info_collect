
Hecht's <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm">pneumonia</a>:  Also known as giant cell <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm">pneumonia</a>, this is a deadly but fortunately rare  complication of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/measles_rubeola/article.htm">measles</a>. It tends to strike children who are immunodeficient as from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/leukemia/article.htm">leukemia</a> or HIV/AIDS. The postmortem examination (autopsy) shows multinucleated giant cells lining the alveoli (air sacs) of the lungs. Named for the early 20th-century Austrian pathologist Victor Hecht.
