
Antipyretic: Something that reduces <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a> or quells it. There are 3 classes of antipyretic medications that are sold OTC (over-the-counter) without prescription:
<ul>
<li>Salicylates -- aspirin (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/acetylsalicylic_acid/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">acetylsalicylic acid</a>), choline salicylate (Arthropan), magnesium salicylate (Arthriten), and sodium salicylate (Scot-Tussin Original); </li>
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/acetaminophen/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">Acetaminophen</a> (Tylenol); and </li>
<li>Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) -- <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ibuprofen/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">ibuprofen</a> (Advil),  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/naproxen/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">naproxen</a> (Naprosyn, Aleve),
and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ketoprofen/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">ketoprofen</a>. </li></ul>
From anti-, against + the Greek pyretos, fever from pyr, fire. The same root gives rise to pyrexia, a medical term for fever.
