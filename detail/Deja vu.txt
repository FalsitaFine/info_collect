
Deja vu: (In French, dejà vu means "already seen." and the word dejà has an acute accent on the e and a grave accent on the à but we have omitted the accents from the entry term for the sake of the English-speaking search engine.) 

Dejà vu is a disquieting feeling of having been 
somewhere or done something before, even though one has 
not. Although most people have experienced this feeling at 
some time or another, in certain people such sensations of deja vu are part of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5442">seizure</a> or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11671">migraine aura</a>; while in others 
they are the seizure phenomenon itself. 
See also <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4069">Jamais vu</a>.
