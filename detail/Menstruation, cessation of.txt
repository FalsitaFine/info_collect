
Menstruation, cessation of: The ending of a woman's menstrual cycles. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/menstruation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Menstruation</a> ends naturally in middle age with the onset of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/menopause/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">menopause</a>. It can also end suddenly as a result of induced menopause. Cessation of menstruation in nonmenopausal women may be due to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a>, illness, disorders of the hypothalamus or pituitary gland, medication, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stress/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">stress</a>, overexercise, or malnutrition, among other causes. In particular, it can be a symptom of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/anorexia_nervosa/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">anorexia</a>, signaling potentially dangerous changes in the body's hormonal system. Cessation of menstruation in women who have established a regular menstrual cycle, or in girls or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/teenagers/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">teens</a> who show other signs of anorexia, is a cause for medical concern. Also known as amenhorrhea.
