
Dystonia, Segawa: An important variant form of dopa-responsive <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dystonia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dystonia</a> (DRD). DRD typically begins in childhood or adolescence with progressive difficulty in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/walking/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">walking</a> and, in some cases, with spasticity. DRD, by definition, can be successfully treated with dopa.
In Segawa dystonia, the symptoms fluctuate during the day from
relative mobility in the morning to increasingly worse disability in the afternoon and evening and after <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">exercise</a>.
