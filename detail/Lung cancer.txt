
Lung cancer: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Cancer</a> of the major organ of respiration - the lung. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/lung_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Lung cancer</a> is one of the most common forms of cancer. Since the majority of lung cancer is diagnosed at a relatively late stage, only 10% of all lung cancer patients are ultimately cured. Eight out of 10 lung cancers are due to tobacco smoke. Most lung cancers are classified as either small cell or non-small cell cancers. Persistent <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chronic_cough/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cough</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/bloody_sputum/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">bloody sputum</a> can be symptoms of lung cancer. Lung cancer can be diagnosed based on examination of sputum, or tissue examination with biopsy using  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/bronchoscopy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">bronchoscopy</a>, needle through the chest wall, or surgical excision.
