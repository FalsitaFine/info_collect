
Bone, zygomatic: The bone that forms the prominence of the cheek. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6073" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">zygomatic bone</a> is also known as the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6071" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">zygoma</a>, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6072" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">zygomatic arch</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4254" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">malar</a> bone, yoke bone. The word "zygomatic" comes from the Greek "zygon" meaning a yoke (as for oxen).
