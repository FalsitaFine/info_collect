
Ectodermal dysplasia: A genetic disorder in which the skin and associated structures (the hair, nails, teeth, and sweat glands) develop abnormally. X-linked <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=16310" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anhidrotic</a> (nonsweating) ectodermal dysplasia is most common; because it is an X-linked trait, it mainly affects males. There is also an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11974" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">autosomal dominant</a> form that affects both males and females. The term <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20841" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ectodermal</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3142" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">dysplasia</a> refers to the abnormal development (dysplasia) of structures derived from one of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8591" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">germ cell</a> layers in the embryo (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20835" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ectoderm</a>).
