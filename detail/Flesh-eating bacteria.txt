
Flesh-eating bacteria: A popular media term for a type of strep bacteria (group A <i>streptococcus</i>) which rapidly destroys tissue and left untreated causes death. Surgical <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=38490" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">excision</a> of dead and infected tissue is usually necessary to help control the infection. The rapid spread and destruction of tissue caused by these bacteria is thought due to a combination of factors related to a protective coat and other substances produced by the bacteria.
