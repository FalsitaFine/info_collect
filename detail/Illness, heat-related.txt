
Illness, heat-related: A person with symptoms including <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">headache</a>, nausea, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fatigue</a> after exposure to heat probably has some measure of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hyperthermia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">heat-related illness</a>. It is important to recognize the difference between the very serious condition known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heat_stroke/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">heat stroke</a> and other heat-related illnesses. Persons experiencing any of these symptoms should consult a doctor.   The heat-related illnesses include:  <u>Heat <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stress/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">stress</a>: </u> Heat stress occurs when a strain is placed on the body as a result of hot weather.
