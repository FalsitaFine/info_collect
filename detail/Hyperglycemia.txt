
Hyperglycemia: A high blood sugar. An elevated level 
specifically of the sugar glucose in the blood. 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hyperglycemia/article.htm">Hyperglycemia</a> is often found in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm">diabetes mellitus</a>. It occurs when 
the body does not have enough <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/insulin/article.htm">insulin</a> or cannot use the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/insulin/article.htm">insulin</a> it 
has to turn glucose into energy. Hyperglycemia may also occur 
in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cushings_syndrome/article.htm">Cushing's syndrome</a> and other conditions. 
The signs of hyperglycemia are polydipsia (a great thirst), 
polyuria (a need to urinate often), and a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dry_mouth/article.htm">dry mouth</a>. 
The term "hyperglycemia" comes from the Greek "hyper-" = high, 
over, beyond, above + "glykys" = sweet + "haima" = blood. High 
sweetness (sugar) in the blood.
