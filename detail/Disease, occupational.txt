
Disease, occupational: A disease due to a factor in a person's occupation. For example, lung disease in miners. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18430" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Occupational medicine</a> was founded by the Italian physician Bernardino Ramazzini (1633-1714). His De Morbis Artificium (On Artificially Caused Diseases) published in 1700 was the first systematic study of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18427" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">occupational disease</a>. He recognized the relationship between metals such as lead and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18819" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">antimony</a> and the symptoms of poisoning in painters and other artisans exposed to them. Ramazzini also recognized diseases associated with a number of other occupations including lung disease in miners.
