
Farsightedness: An error of refraction in the human eye that causes light rays to focus behind the retina instead of on it. A person who is farsighted has normal vision at a distance but has trouble focusing on nearby objects. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/farsightedness/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Farsightedness</a> can be corrected with refractive lenses'either glasses or contact lenses'and in some cases by surgery. Also known as hyperopia.
