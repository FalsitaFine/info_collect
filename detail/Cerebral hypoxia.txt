
Cerebral hypoxia: a decrease in the amount of oxygen delivered to the brain. This can be due to a decreased amount of oxygen in the blood, a decrease in the pumping power of the heart, or any type of obstruction to blood flow. In mild cases, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypoxia_and_hypoxemia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cerebral hypoxia</a> causes poor judgment, inattentiveness, and problems with control of movement. In more serious cases, there is loss of breathing and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/coma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">coma</a>, which can be fatal. There are multiple causes of cerebral hypoxia. Some of the more common causes include 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sudden_cardiac_death/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cardiac arrest</a>, choking, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stroke_symptoms_and_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">stroke</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/low_blood_pressure/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">low blood pressure</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/shock/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">shock</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/drowning/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">drowning</a>, drug overdose, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/paralysis/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">paralysis</a> of the breathing muscles in conditions such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a> (ALS), strangulation, smoke inhalation, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/carbon_monoxide_poisoning_symptoms_and_signs/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">carbon monoxide poisoning</a>. Cerebral hypoxia can also occur at birth and lead to brain damage in the newborn.
