
Enoxaparin: A low-molecular-weight version of heparin that acts like heparin as an anticoagulant medication. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/enoxaparin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">Enoxaparin</a> is used to prevent thromboembolic complications (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blood_clots/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">blood clots</a> that travel from their site of origin through the bloodstream to clog another vessel) and in the early treatment of blood clots in the lungs (pulmonary embolisms).
