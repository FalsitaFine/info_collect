
Intrauterine insemination (IUI): A procedure in which a fine catheter (tube)
is inserted through the cervix (the natural opening of the uterus) into the
uterus (the womb) to deposit a sperm sample directly into the uterus. The
purpose of IUI is to achieve fertilization and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a>. IUI is a
relatively simple procedure. "Intrauterine" is within the uterus.
"Insemination" is from "in-" + "semen," seed = seed in. So, "intrauterine
insemination" = seed in the uterus.
