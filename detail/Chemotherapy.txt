
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chemotherapy/article.htm">Chemotherapy</a>: 1. In the original sense, a chemical that binds to and specifically kills microbes or tumor cells. The term chemotherapy was coined in this regard by  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31950">Paul Ehrlich</a> (1854-1915).<br>
2. In oncology, drug <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10897">therapy</a> for cancer. Also called "chemo" for short.
Most cancer chemotherapeutic drugs are given <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4064">IV</a> (into a vein) or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3904">IM</a> (into muscle). Some anticancer agents are taken orally (by mouth). Chemotherapy is usually systemic treatment, meaning that the drugs flow through the bloodstream to nearly every part of the body.
