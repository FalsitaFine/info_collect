
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3230" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Empyema</a>: Empyema is a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10778" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">condition</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> which <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5140" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pus</a> and fluid from infected <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5800" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tissue</a> collects in a body cavity. The name comes from the Greek word empyein meaning pus-producing (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20665" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">suppurate</a>). Empyema is most often used to refer to collections of pus in the space around the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4209" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">lungs</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25371" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pleural</a> cavity), but sometimes refers to similar collections in the gall <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2472" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bladder</a> or the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4822" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pelvic</a> cavity. Empyema may have a number of causes but is most frequently a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25405" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">complication</a> of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4962" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pneumonia</a>. Its <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2964" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">development</a> can be divided into three phases: an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2133" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">acute</a> phase in which the body cavity fills with a thin fluid containing some pus; a second <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5543" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">stage</a> in which the fluid thickens and a fibrous, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18597" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">coagulation</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6554" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">protein</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3450" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">fibrin</a>) begins to accumulate within the cavity, and a third or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2728" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chronic</a> stage in which the lung or other <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=21288" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">organ</a> is encased within a thick covering of fibrous material.
