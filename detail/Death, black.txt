
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33438" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Death</a>, black: The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2471" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">black plague</a> or the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4927" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">plague</a>. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">In</a> 14th century Europe, the victims of the "black plague" had bleeding below the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7901" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">skin</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8265" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">subcutaneous</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=14263" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hemorrhage</a>) which made darkened ("blackened") their bodies. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2470" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">black death</a> swept recurrently through Europe, killing al least 1/3 its population in the middle of the 14th century.
