
La Montagne, John: (1945-2004) Medical scientist and administrator who helped develop numerous vaccines and made significant contributions to the international effort against emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases. He played a central role in the organization of the Multilateral Initiative on <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/malaria_facts/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Malaria</a>, an international effort involving agencies in many countries. He was involved in the development of the swine <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/influenza/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">flu</a> vaccine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pertussis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">whooping cough</a> (pertussis) vaccine, AID medicines, and vaccines against childhood <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pneumonia</a>. A native of Mexico City, Mexico, LaMontagne received his PhD from Tulane University in 1971 and in 1976 came to National Institute of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/allergy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Allergy</a> and Infectious Diseases (NIAID) of the National Institutes of Health (NIH) where he rose to deputy director. La Montagne died suddenly in Mexico City at age 61.
