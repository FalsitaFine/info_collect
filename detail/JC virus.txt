
JC virus: A virus that commonly causes infections of no consequence in children with a normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3907" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immune system</a>, but which is responsible for an infection of the brain and spinal cord called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11209" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy</a> (PML) in people with AIDS and other forms of immune system impairment. Abbreviated JVC. The JC virus is a human polyomavirus. It is a small virus with a closed circular genome consisting of double-stranded DNA. The letters JC are the initials of a patient with PML from whose brain the virus was first isolated.
