
Fava bean: The broad bean to which many people react
adversely with an acute hemolytic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/anemia/article.htm">anemia</a> with sudden breakup of red blood cells (see
Favism). Fava beans look like large tan lima beans. They are popular in Mediterranean and
Middle Eastern cuisines, are eaten raw when very young, cooked in soups and many other
dishes, and made into fava brittle (like peanut brittle) as candy. The botanical name for
fava is Vicia fava. Fava is Italian for bean and refers specifically to the broad bean.
Fava beans are the main commercial source of the drug L-DOPA.
