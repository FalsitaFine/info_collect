
Alongshan virus: This is a segmented RNA virus first identified in 2017 in 
China. Alongshan virus (ALSV) belongs to the jingmenvirus group of the family 
<i>Flaviviridae</i>. In northern China, patients who had this viral infection 
also experienced <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a> and illness. Medical professionals believe the virus 
spreads via tick bites. In addition to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fever/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">fever</a> and a history of tick bites, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">headache</a> was a symptom of infection, similar to other tick-borne <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/encephalitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">encephalitis</a> virus infections.
