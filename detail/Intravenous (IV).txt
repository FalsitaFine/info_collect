
Intravenous (IV): 1) Into a vein. Intravenous (IV)   medications are a solutions administered directly into the venous <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2735" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">circulation</a> via a syringe or   intravenous catheter (tube). 2) The actual solution that is   administered intravenously. 3) The device used to administer an   intravenous solution, such as the familiar IV drip.
