
Magic bullet: Or, sometimes, silver bullet. 1. The perfect drug to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31243">cure</a> a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3011">disease</a> with no danger of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5489">side effects</a>. The term magic bullet was first used in this sense by the German scientist <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31950">Paul Ehrlich</a> to describe <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/liver_anatomy_and_function/article.htm01">antibody</a> and, later, the drug salvarsan that he created to treat <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5689">syphilis</a>. 
2.  In general, a magical solution to any vexing problem.
