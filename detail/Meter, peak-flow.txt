
Meter, peak-flow: This hand-held device measures air flow (how fast air is blown out of the lungs). Patients can use peak-flow meters to measure their own air flow regularly. The use of a peak-flow meter allows patients to obtain a much earlier indication of an oncoming attack, for example, of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise-induced_asthma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">exercise-induced asthma</a>, a type of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/asthma_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">asthma</a> typically triggered by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">exercise</a>.
The "peak flow" refers to the maximum flow rate of air breathed out during forced expiration.
