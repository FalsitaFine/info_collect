
Human Genome Project: Begun formally in 1990, the US Human Genome Project was an international effort coordinated by the US. Its goals included the identification and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5465" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">sequencing</a> (ordering) of all the bases in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3818" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">human genome</a>. US participation in this monumental undertaking was supported by funds from the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9786" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">National Institutes of Health</a> (NIH) and the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2946" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Department of Energy</a> (DOE). The project was successful.
