
Non-cystic fibrosis bronchiectasis: a type of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/bronchiectasis_acquired_congenital/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">bronchiectasis</a> (permanent dilation of the airways of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/image-collection/lungs_picture/picture.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="img">lungs</a>) that arises as a result of chronic inflammation and is not due to the genetic condition <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cystic_fibrosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cystic fibrosis</a>. While cystic fibrosis is a known cause of bronchiectasis, a number of other conditions can also cause the condition. In a patient with bronchiectasis, a search for a cause of the condition should be carried out, and this usually includes tests to rule out cystic fibrosis. Symptoms include a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cough/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">chronic cough</a> and sputum production. Imaging studies show widening of the airways, often with thickening of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/image-collection/airway_picture/picture.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="img">airway</a> walls.
