
Berlin Heart: The name of a company in Berlin, Germany and of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=19095" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ventricular assist device</a> it makes. The device works by helping the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9124" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">right ventricle</a> of the heart to pump blood to the lungs and the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9127" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">left ventricle</a> to pump blood to the body. The Berlin Heart comes in various sizes for a range of patients, including newborn babies. The bulk of the device is extracorporeal (outside the body). Only the tubes are implanted. They emerge from small openings to enter the pump, a small round chamber. The system is run by a laptop computer. The Berlin Heart is intended to be used as a bridge to recovery or as a bridge to a transplant.
