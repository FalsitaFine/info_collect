
MASS syndrome: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3719">heritable</a> disorder of connective 
tissue characterized by involvement of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4410">mitral valve</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2295">aorta</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5503">skeleton</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7901">skin</a>. Hence, the acronym MASS (Mitral valve, Aorta, Skeleton, Skin). 
The MASS syndrome is due to a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4471">mutation</a> in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33167">FBN1</a>, the fibrillin 1 gene. The same gene as is mutated in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4282">Marfan syndrome</a>.
