
Dyskeratosis congenita: An inherited cause of bone-marrow failure, dyskeratosis congenita is a syndrome characterized by abnormal excess skin pigmentation, abnormal or absent nails, and mucosal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/leukoplakia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">leukoplakia</a> (white premalignant areas on the lips and conjunctiva of the eyes and in the mouth, anus, and urethra).
