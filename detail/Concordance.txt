
Concordance: 1.  The presence of any given condition such as HIV in both members of a couple.<br> 2. In genetics, the presence of a phenotype such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/asthma_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">asthma</a> in both members of a twin pair.<br>
3. In clinical care, agreement between physician and patient.<br>
In all sense, concordance is as opposed to discordance. From the Latin concordare, to agree.</br></br></br>
