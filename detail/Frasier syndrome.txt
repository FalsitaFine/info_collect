
Frasier syndrome: A condition characterized by the presence of an XY <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20692" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">sex chromosome</a> constitution and undermasculinized external genitalia that may range from ambiguous in appearance to normal-looking <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33682" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">female genitalia</a>. There is also kidney disease (glomerulosclerosis) and gonadal tumors (gonadoblastoma). Frasier syndrome is caused by point mutations in the WT1 gene (in the intron 9 donor splice site).
