
Miller-Dieker syndrome: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2820" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">congenital malformation</a> syndrome characterized by lissencephaly ("smooth brain") and a characteristic facial appearance with a prominent forehead with bitemporal hollowing, short nose with upturned nares, thickened upper lip with a thin vermilion upper border, widely spaced eyes, low ears, and small jaw. Associated features include mental retardation, epilepsy, pre- and postnatal growth retardation, and reduced life span. There may also be multiple abnormalities of the brain, kidneys, heart, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25976" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">gastrointestinal tract</a>.  MDS is a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13894" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">contiguous gene syndrome</a>. It is due to deletion of several adjacent genes from 17p (the short arm of chromosome 17). The syndrome is named for JQ Miller who described it in 1963 and   H Dieker who in 1969 emphasized that it should be termed the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23948" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">lissencephaly syndrome</a> because there are malformations beyond the brain.
