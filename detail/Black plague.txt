
Black plague: In 14<sup>th</sup>-century Europe, the victims of the
"<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/plague_facts/article.htm">black plague</a>" had bleeding below the skin (subcutaneous hemorrhage) which made
darkened ("blackened") their bodies. Black plague can lead to "black
death" characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/gangrene/article.htm">gangrene</a> of the fingers, toes, and nose. Black plague is
caused by a bacterium (Yersinia pestis) which is transmitted to humans from infected rats
by the oriental rat flea.
