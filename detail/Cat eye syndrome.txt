
Cat eye syndrome: A constitutional chromosome abnormality (one that is present at or before birth) with multiple malformations characterized by the combination of a cat-like slit of the iris of the eye (vertical <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12064" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">coloboma</a>) and no anal opening (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=16239" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">anal atresia</a>). Frequently there are also down slanting eye slits (palpebral fissures), tissue tags or pits just in front of the ears, heart malformations and kidney anomalies.
