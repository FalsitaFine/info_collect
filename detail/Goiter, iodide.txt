
Goiter, iodide: A goiter caused by prolonged intake of too much iodine that results in abnormally low thyroid activity (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypothyroidism/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hypothyroidism</a>). Certain foods and medications contain large amounts of iodine. Examples include seaweed; iodine-rich expectorants (Brand names: SSKI and Lugol solution) used in the treatment of cough, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/asthma_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">asthma</a>, and chronic pulmonary disease; and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/amiodarone/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">amiodarone</a> (brand name: Cardorone), an iodine-rich medication used in the control of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heart_rhythm_disorders/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abnormal heart rhythms</a>.
