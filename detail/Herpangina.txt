
Herpangina: A viral illness in which small, painful ulcers and sores are found inside the mouth. It is accompanied by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sore_throat_pharyngitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sore throat</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a>. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/herpangina/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Herpangina</a> is most common in children aged 3-10 but can occur at any age. Herpangina is usually caused by Coxsackie group A viruses. The illness typically resolves within a week with no complications.
