
Monoclonal antibody: An antibody produced by a single clone of cells. A monoclonal antibody is therefore a single pure type of antibody. Monoclonal antibodies can be made in large quantities in the laboratory and are a cornerstone of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3941" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immunology</a>. Monoclonal antibodies are increasingly coming into use as therapeutic agents.
