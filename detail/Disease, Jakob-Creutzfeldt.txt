
Disease, Jakob-Creutzfeldt: A transmissible degenerative brain disorder technically termed spongiform <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/encephalopathy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">encephalopathy</a>.
Eating "mad cow" meat or squirrel brain can lead to Jakob-Creutzfeldt-like
disease. Better known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/creutzfeldt-jakob_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease</a> (CJD). A dementing disease of the brain, believed due to an unconventional, transmissible agent (a prion). Symptoms of CJD include forgetfulness, nervousness, jerky trembling hand movements, unsteady gait, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/muscle_spasms/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">muscle spasms</a>, chronic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dementia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dementia</a>, balance disorder, and loss of facial expression. CJD is
classified as a spongiform encephalopathy. Most cases occur randomly (sporadically), but
inherited forms exist. There is neither treatment nor cure for CJD. Other names for CJD
include Creutzfeldt-Jakob syndrome, Jakob's disease, and spastic pseudoparalysis.
