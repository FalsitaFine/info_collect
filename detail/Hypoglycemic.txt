
Hypoglycemic: Relating to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypoglycemia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hypoglycemia</a>, an abnormally low level of the sugar <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glucose/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">glucose</a> in the blood, usually a complication of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a>, in which the body does not produce enough   <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/insulin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">insulin</a> to fully metabolize glucose.      Hypoglycemic symptoms include tiredness, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dizziness_dizzy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dizziness</a>, confusion, increased   heart rate, and a cold, clammy feeling.   The treatment of hypoglycemia is careful diet,   including eating small meals or snacks throughout the day; or by   changing the dose or timing of insulin or other drugs that affect   blood sugar levels.  Severe hypoglycemia must be treated immediately with sugar or a high carbohydrate snack.
