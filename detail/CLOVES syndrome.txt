
CLOVES syndrome: a rare disorder characterized by defects in the vascular (blood vessels) system and overgrowth of certain body tissues. CLOVES stands for congenital lipomatous (fatty) overgrowth, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/image-collection/vascular_malformations_on_foot_picture/picture.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="img">vascular malformations</a>, epidermal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/skin_problems_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">nevi</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/scoliosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">scoliosis</a>/skeletal/spinal anomalies. Symptoms include fatty masses of tissue that are often covered with a pink <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birthmarks_and_other_skin_pigmentation_problems/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">birthmark</a>. These masses are found in the back, flanks, axilla, abdomen, and buttocks. Dilated blood vessels and other changes in the blood and lymphatic channels are present along with defects in the arms and legs, kidneys, and skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birthmarks_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">birthmarks</a>. CLOVES is a non-hereditary (not <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/genetic_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">inherited</a>) disorder that is caused by an acquired defect in a gene known as PIK3CA. Symptoms are typically observed at birth. Both males and females are equally affected.
