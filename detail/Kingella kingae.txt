
Kingella kingae: A type of bacteria that has emerged from obscurity to become known as an important cause of invasive infections in young children. These infections include especially <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/septic_arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">septic arthritis</a> (infection of joints), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/osteomyelitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">osteomyelitis</a> (infection of bone), spondylodiscitis (infection of the spine and discs), bacteremia (infection of the bloodstream), and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/endocarditis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">endocarditis</a> (infection of the heart valves). Less frequently, Kingella kingae is responsible for lower respiratory tract infections and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/encephalitis_and_meningitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">meningitis</a>.
