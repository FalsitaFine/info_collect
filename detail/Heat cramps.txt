
Heat cramps: Painful <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/muscle_spasms/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">muscle spasms</a> in the abdomen, arms, or legs following strenuous activity. The skin is usually moist and cool and the pulse is normal or slightly raised. Body temperature is mostly normal. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heat_cramps/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Heat cramps</a> often are caused by a lack of salt in the body, but salt replacement should not be considered without advice from a physician. Heat cramps usually affect people who sweat a lot during strenuous activity. The sweating depletes the body's salt and moisture. The low salt level in the muscles causes painful cramps. Heat cramps may also be a symptom of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/heat_exhaustion/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">heat exhaustion</a>.
