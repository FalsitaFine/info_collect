
Cataract:  A clouding or loss of transparency of the lens in the eye 
as a result of tissue breakdown and protein clumping. There are many causes of 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cataracts/article.htm"> cataracts</a>, including aging, cortisone medication, trauma, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm">diabetes</a>, and other 
diseases. Cataracts affect most people who live into an old age. Symptoms 
include double or blurred vision and sensitivity to light and glare. A physician 
can diagnose cataracts by examining the eyes with a viewing instrument. 
Sunglasses can help to prevent cataracts.
