
Hepatic steatosis: a term used to refer to the buildup of fat within cells of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_anatomy_and_function/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">liver</a>. This is a general term that simply refers to fat in the liver. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatty_liver/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Fatty liver</a> can be due to alcohol use disorder and heavy consumption of alcohol as well as a group of other conditions referred to as nonalcoholic fatty <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">liver disease</a> (NAFLD). Nonalcoholic fatty <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_disease_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">liver disease</a> is the most common form of chronic liver disease in the US, affecting around 80 to 100 million people. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/obesity_weight_loss/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Obesity</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/type_2_diabetes/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">type 2 diabetes</a> increase the risk of developing nonalcoholic fatty liver disease. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatty_liver/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Nonalcoholic steatohepatitis</a> (NASH) is a potentially serious type of NAFLD associated with liver inflammation that can develop into scarring (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cirrhosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cirrhosis</a>), liver damage, which can lead to 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_cancer_hepatocellular_carcinoma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">liver cancer</a>. Fatty liver itself typically does not cause symptoms, but in some people, it may cause <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tiredness</a>, an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_disease_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">enlarged liver</a>, and right upper <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a>.
