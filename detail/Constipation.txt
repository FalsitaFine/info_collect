
Constipation: Infrequent and frequently incomplete bowel movements. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/constipation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Constipation</a> is the opposite of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a> and is commonly caused by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/irritable_bowel_syndrome_ibs_triggers_prevention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">irritable bowel syndrome</a> (IBS), diverticulosis, and medications. Paradoxically, constipation can also be caused by overuse of laxatives. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colon_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Colon cancer</a> can also narrow the colon and thereby cause constipation. A high-<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fiber/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">fiber</a> diet can frequently relieve constipation. If the diet is not helpful, medical evaluation is warranted.
