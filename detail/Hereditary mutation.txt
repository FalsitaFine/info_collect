
Hereditary mutation: A gene change that occurs in a germ cell (an egg or a sperm) and is then incorporated into every cell in the developing body of the new organism. Hereditary mutations play a role in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a>, as, for example, in the eye <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/retinoblastoma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">retinoblastoma</a> and Wilms' tumor of the kidney. Also known as germline mutation.
