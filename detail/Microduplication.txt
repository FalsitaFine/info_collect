
Microduplication: The gain of a tiny piece of a chromosome, a piece so small its presence is not apparent on ordinary examination (using a regular light microscope to look at chromosomes prepared in the usual fashion). The detection of microduplications requires special techniques such as high-resolution <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15921" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chromosome banding</a>, molecular chromosome analysis (with FISH), or molecular genetic analysis. 
Disorders caused by microduplications include  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13609" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease</a> type 1A (CMT1A) and the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25474" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">microduplication 22q11.2 syndrome. </a>
The prefix "micro-" is derived from the Greek "mikros" meaning small. It diminishes whatever it precedes.
