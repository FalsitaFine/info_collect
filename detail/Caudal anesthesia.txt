
Caudal anesthesia: Anesthesia produced by injection of a local anesthetic into the caudal canal, the sacral portion of the spinal canal. Caudal anesthesia is used to provide anesthesia and analgesia (pain relief) below the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5899" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">umbilicus</a>. It may be the sole anesthetic or combined with general anesthesia. Also known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22585" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">caudal epidural anesthesia</a> or a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22583" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">caudal block</a>.
