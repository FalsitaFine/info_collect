
Aquagenic urticaria: a skin reaction that results from contact with water. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hives/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Urticaria</a> is the medical term for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hives/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hives</a>, a skin reaction characterized by red, raised, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/itch/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">itchy</a> welts on the skin. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/image-collection/urticaria_picture/picture.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="img">Urticaria</a> can be related to an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/allergies_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">allergic reaction</a>, but its cause is not determined in many cases. The skin changes can come and go, change size, and move to different locations in the body. Symptoms and signs of aquagenic urticaria are similar to ordinary hives. The exact reason why water provokes this reaction in some people is not well understood.
