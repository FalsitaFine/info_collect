
Hair of the dog: An alcoholic chaser reputed to relieve a hangover, usually needed the morning after imbibing too much alcohol. There is no scientific evidence as to the efficacy of this time-honored treatment for a hangover. The saying originated in the belief that a cure for hydrophobia (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rabies_virus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rabies</a>) or any disease contracted from a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dog_bite_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dog bite</a> consisted of taking a hair of the dog that bit you and placing it in the wound.
