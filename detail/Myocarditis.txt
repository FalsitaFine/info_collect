
Myocarditis: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3979">Inflammation</a> of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/polymyalgia_rheumatica/article.htm7">myocardium</a>, the heart muscle.
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/myocarditis/article.htm">Myocarditis</a> can be caused by a number of different conditions. The most common cause is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12923">infection</a> of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9658">heart muscle</a> by a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5997">virus</a>. The virus causes the initial heart muscle inflammation. After the initial <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11557">viral infection</a> subsides, the body's <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3907">immune system</a> continues to inflict inflammatory damage on the heart muscles, prolonging the myocarditis, a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5054">process</a> termed <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18985">autoimmunity</a>. Other causes of myocarditis include such as a disease called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13430">sarcoidosis</a>, various <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/liver_anatomy_and_function/article.htm02">autoimmune</a> diseases such as 
systemic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8064">lupus</a>, and certain drugs.
