
APECED (autoimmune polyendocrinopathy-candidiasis-ectodermal dystrophy): A genetic autoimmune disease with an extraordinary array of clinical features but characterized most often by at least 2 of the following 3 findings: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypoparathyroidism/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hypoparathyroidism</a> -- under function of the parathyroid glands which control calcium, candidiasis (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/yeast_infection_in_women_and_men/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">yeast infection</a>), and adrenal insufficiency (under function of the adrenal gland). APECED was the first systemic (bodywide) autoimmune disease found due to a defect in a single gene.
