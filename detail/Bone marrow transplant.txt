
Bone marrow transplant: A procedure in which <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2502">bone marrow</a> that is diseased or damaged is replaced with healthy bone marrow. The bone marrow to be replaced may be deliberately destroyed by high doses of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chemotherapy/article.htm">chemotherapy</a> and/or 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/radiation_therapy/article.htm">radiation therapy</a>. 
The replacement marrow may be the patient's own marrow or it may come from another person. If the marrow comes from the patient. it is called an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13210">autologous</a> bone marrow transplant. If the marrow is from an identical twin, it is termed a syngeneic transplant. And if the marrow is from a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3106">donor</a> who is not an identical twin, the procedure is an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25266">allogeneic</a> bone marrow transplant. Most bone marrow transplants today are allogeneic.
Bone marrow transplant is abbreviated BMT.
