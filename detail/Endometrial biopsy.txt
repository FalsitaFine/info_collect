
Endometrial biopsy: A common procedure for sampling the inner lining of the uterus (the endometrium). <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/endometrial_biopsy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">Endometrial biopsy</a> is usually done to learn the cause of abnormal uterine bleeding, although it may be used to determine the cause of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/infertility/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">infertility</a>, test for uterine infections, and monitor the response to certain medications. The main problems resulting from endometrial biopsy are cramping and pain. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/vaginal_bleeding/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Vaginal bleeding</a>, infection, and, very rarely, perforation of the uterus can also occur.
