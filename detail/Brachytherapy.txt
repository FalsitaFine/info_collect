
Brachytherapy: Radiation treatment given by placing radioactive material directly in or near the target, which is often a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a>. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/brachytherapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">Brachytherapy</a> for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prostate_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">prostate cancer</a>, for example, is also called interstitial <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/radiation_therapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">radiation therapy</a> or seed implantation. In brachytherapy for prostate <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a>, radioactive seeds are implanted in the prostate. The seeds might be titanium-encased pellets that contain the radioisotope iodine-125.
