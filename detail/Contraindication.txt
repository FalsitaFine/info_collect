
Contraindication: A condition which makes a particular treatment or procedure potentially inadvisable. A contraindication may be absolute or relative.   <ul> <li>An absolute contraindication is a situation which makes a particular treatment or procedure absolutely inadvisable. In children, for example, aspirin is almost always contraindicated because of the danger that aspirin will cause Reye syndrome.     </li></ul><ul> <li>A relative contraindication is a condition which makes a particular treatment or procedure possibly inadvisable. For example, X-rays in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a> are relatively contraindicated (because of concern for the developing fetus) unless the X-rays are absolutely necessary.  </li></ul> Contraindications often highlight the balance of risk versus benefit of a particular treatment or procedure.
