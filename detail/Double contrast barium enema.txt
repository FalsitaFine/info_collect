
Double contrast barium enema: A series of x-rays of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2787">colon</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5253">rectum</a> taken after the patient is given an enema, followed by an injection of air. The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25935">barium</a> outlines the intestines on the x-rays, allowing many <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22433">abnormal</a> growths to be visible. See: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2425">Barium enema</a>.
