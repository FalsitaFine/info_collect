
Myxedema coma: A rare and life-threatening complication of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hypothyroidism/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hypothyroidism</a> (low levels of thyroid hormones in the bloodstream) characterized by loss of brain function (coma). Before a   patient develops myxedema   coma, features of hypothyroidism are usually present and may have gone   unsuspected for a long period of time, including <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fatigue</a>, lethargy, intolerance   to cold, mental and mood changes, headaches                                  , <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/depression/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">depression</a>, weight gain, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/constipation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">constipation</a>, and   changes in menstrual cycles     . When <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/myxedema_coma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">myxedema coma</a> develops, the body temperature is typically abnormally low, and there is diffuse swelling (excess fluid or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/edema/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">edema</a>) throughout the body along with the severe changes in brain function.
