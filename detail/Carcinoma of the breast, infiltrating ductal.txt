
Carcinoma of the breast, infiltrating ductal: One of several recognized specific patterns of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a> of the breast, so named because it begins in the cells that form the ducts of the breast. It is the most common form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/breast_cancer_facts_stages/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">breast cancer</a>. On a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/mammogram/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">mammogram</a>, invasive ductal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer_101_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">carcinoma</a> is usually visualized as a mass with fine spikes radiating from the edges (speculation). It can sometimes be felt as a firm lump in the breast
