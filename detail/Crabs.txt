
Crabs: Slang for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pubic_lice_crabs/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pubic lice</a>, parasitic insects that can infest in the genital area of humans. Pubic lice are usually spread through <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sexual_sex_problems_in_men/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sexual</a> contact. Rarely, infestation can be spread through contact with an infested person's bed linens, towels, or clothes. The key symptom of pubic lice is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/itch/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">itching</a> in the genital area. Lice eggs (nits) or crawling lice can be seen with the naked eye.
