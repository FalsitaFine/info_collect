
Caspase 3: An enzyme that plays a key role in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11288" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">programmed cell death</a>, or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11287" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">apoptosis</a>. Caspase 3 has been called the "henchman that goes around and executes the cell." It is a member of the family of asparate-specific cysteinyl proteases and is also known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23607" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">caspase-3</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23608" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">CPP32</a>, apopain, or YAMA.
