
IBS (irritable bowel syndrome): A common gastrointestinal disorder involving an abnormal condition of gut contractions (motility) and gut sensation (hypersensitivity)  characterized by   <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a>, bloating, mucous in stools, and irregular bowel   habits with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/constipation/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">constipation</a> or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a> or alternating  diarrhea and constipation, symptoms that tend to be chronic and to wax   and wane over the  years. Although <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/irritable_bowel_syndrome_ibs_triggers_prevention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">irritable bowel syndrome</a> can cause chronic recurrent discomfort, it does   not lead to any serious abdominal organ problems.   Making the diagnosis usually involves suspecting and making a positive diagnosis and excluding other   illnesses. Treatment is directed toward relief of symptoms and includes changes in diet (eating moderately high <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fiber/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">fiber</a> and avoiding <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/caffeine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">caffeine</a>, milk products and sweeteners),    <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/exercise/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sub">exercise</a>, relaxation techniques, and   medications.   Also called spastic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/colitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">colitis</a>, mucus colitis, nervous   colon syndrome.
