
Chronic glomerulonephritis: One of a group of kidney diseases characterized by long-term inflammation and scarring of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/kidney_disease_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">glomeruli</a> (microscopic structures in the kidney that filter blood and produce urine). This form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/kidney_disease_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">kidney disease</a> 
usually develops slowly (over years) and may not produce symptoms at the outset. 
When symptoms and signs do appear, they typically include blood in the urine (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blood_in_urine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hematuria</a>), swelling (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/edema/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">edema</a>), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/high_blood_pressure_hypertension/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">high blood pressure</a>, foamy urine (due to protein content), and frequent nighttime urination.
