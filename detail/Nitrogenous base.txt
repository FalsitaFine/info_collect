
Nitrogenous base: A molecule that contains nitrogen and has the chemical properties of a base. The nitrogenous bases in DNA are <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2143" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">adenine</a> (A), guanine (G), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5774" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">thymine</a> (T), and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2908" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cytosine</a> (C). The nitrogenous bases in RNA are the same, with one exception: adenine (A), guanine (G), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13999" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">uracil</a> (U), and cytosine (C).
