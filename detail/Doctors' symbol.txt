
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7229" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Doctors' symbol</a>:  A staff or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10548" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">rod</a> with a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33626" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">snake</a> curled  around it.  This is the Rod of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6985" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Aesculapius</a> (also called Asklepios), the ancient mythical god of medicine.  Asklepios may have been a real person who was renowned for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22858" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">his</a> gentle remedies and humane treatment of the mentally ill. Today, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7946" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">staff of Aesculapius</a> is a commonly used <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39915" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">symbol of medicine</a>.  It is the symbol of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7132" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">American Medical Association</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2214" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">AMA</a>) and many other  medical societies.
