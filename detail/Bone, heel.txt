
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2499" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Bone</a>, heel:  A somewhat rectangular bone at the back of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3499" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">foot</a>. The words "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9013" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">calcaneus</a>" and "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2575" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">calcium</a>" are related. Medically termed the calcaneus. The calcaneus (from the Latin calcaneum, meaning heel or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6420" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">heel bone</a>) is a bone of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25072" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">tarsus</a> of the foot which constitutes the heel. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">In</a> humans, the calcaneus is the largest of the tarsal bones and the largest bone of the foot.
