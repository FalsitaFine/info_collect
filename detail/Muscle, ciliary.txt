
Muscle, ciliary: A circular muscle that  relaxes or tightens the zonules to enable the lens to change shape for focusing.  The zonules are fibers that hold the lens suspended in position and enable it to change shape during <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10528" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">accommodation</a>.
