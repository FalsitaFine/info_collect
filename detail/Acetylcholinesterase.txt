
Acetylcholinesterase: An enzyme that breaks down the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9973" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">neurotransmitter</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23278" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">acetylcholine</a> at the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23280" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">synaptic cleft</a> (the space between two nerve cells) so the next nerve impulse can be transmitted across the synaptic gap. Pesticides of the organophosphate and carbamate types act to paralyze and kill insects by inhibiting their acetylcholinesterase. Abbreviated <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=23282" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">AChE</a>.
