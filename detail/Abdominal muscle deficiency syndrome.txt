
Abdominal muscle deficiency syndrome: Partial or complete absence of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2084" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">abdominal muscles</a> so that the outlines of the intestines are visible through the thin, lax, protruding abdominal wall. Also called the "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10446" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">prune belly syndrome</a>." The full syndrome probably occurs only in males. In addition to the abdominal muscle deficiency, there are genital and urinary abnormalities including dilation (widening) of the urinary tract and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13221" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cryptorchidism</a> (failure for the testes to descend into the scrotum). There is also a form of abdominal muscle deficiency that is associated with narrowing (stenosis) of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24368" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pulmonary artery</a>, mental retardation and deafness. It occurs in both boys and girls.
