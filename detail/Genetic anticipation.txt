
Genetic anticipation: A remarkable phenomenon <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> which a <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31302" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genetic disease</a> appears earlier with each succeeding generation. <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2279" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Anticipation</a> was once thought not to exist in <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15390" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genetics</a>. It was chalked off as a meaningless statistical artifact. However, anticipation has now been proven to occur in a large number of important <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3573" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genetic</a> disorders, including <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3820" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Huntington disease</a> and <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4494" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">myotonic dystrophy</a>. In molecular terms, anticipation is due to the expansion of a <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39352" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">trinucleotide</a> <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25471" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">repeat</a> sequence in the <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13963" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">DNA</a>. This phenomenon also occurs in the <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3517" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">fragile X syndrome</a>, the most common inherited form of <a href="https://www.rxlist.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20174" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mental retardation</a>.
