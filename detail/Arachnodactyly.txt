
Arachnodactyly: Long spider-like fingers and toes, a 
frequent finding in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/marfan_syndrome/article.htm">Marfan syndrome</a>, a heritable disorder of 
connective tissue.
"Arachnodactyly" is derived from the Greek "arachne" (spider) 
and "daktylos" (finger). The Greek word was derived from the 
name "Arachne," a maiden in Greek mythology whom the goddess Athena 
turned into a spider after the girl, a skilled weaver, challenged 
Athena to a weaving contest.
