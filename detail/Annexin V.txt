
Annexin V: A substance that normally forms a shield around certain phospholipid molecules in the blood, blocking their entry into coagulation (clotting) reactions. Annexin V is thought to be a cause of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/antiphospholipid_syndrome/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">antiphospholipid syndrome</a>.
