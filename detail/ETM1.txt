
ETM1: A gene for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=31295" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">essential tremor</a>, uncontrollable shaking of the hands and head and sometimes other parts of the body. The ETM1 gene (also called FET1) was mapped in 1997 to chromosome region 3q13 in a study of Icelandic families. EMT1 is responsible for a fine rapid hand tremor; late tremor of arms, tongue, head, legs and trunk;  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11180" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">dysarthria</a>; mild rigidity; and stiff gait. The onset of symptoms is about 50 years, later in women than men. The condition is progressive. The intellect is spared.
