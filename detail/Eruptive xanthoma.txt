
Eruptive xanthoma: The sudden eruption on the skin of crops of pink papules (firm pea-sized bumps) with a creamy center. They may appear on the hands, feet, arms, legs and buttocks.  The papules may be pruritic (itchy). Eruptive xanthoma is due to high concentrations of plasma <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/triglyceride_test/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">triglycerides</a>, as occurs with uncontrolled <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a>. The xanthomas usually disappear when the underlying condition is treated, as when the diabetes comes under control.
Also known as diabetic xanthoma or tuberoeruptive xanthoma.
