
Human herpesvirus 2 (HHV-2): A herpes virus that causes <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3577" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genital herpes</a>, characterized by sores in the area of the genitalia. Genital herpes is a sexually transmitted disease (STD). This virus, like <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11496" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">human herpesvirus 1</a>, can cause infection of the brain (encephalitis) if the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3907" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">immune system</a> is severely defective or compromised. The treatment of infection with human herpesvirus 2 is by topical or oral anti-viral medication. Human <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3733" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">herpesvirus</a> 2 (HHV-2) is also known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3732" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">herpes simplex type 2</a>.
