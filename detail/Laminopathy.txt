
Laminopathy: A disease due to mutation of the lamin A/C gene. The lamin family of proteins that make up the nuclear lamina, a matrix of protein located next to the inner nuclear membrane.The laminopathies include: Emery-Dreifuss muscular dystrophy type 2, familial partial lipodystrophy, limb girdle muscular dystrophy type 1B, dilated <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cardiomyopathy_hypertrophic/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cardiomyopathy</a>, familial partial lipodystrophy, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/charcot-marie-tooth-disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Charcot-Marie-Tooth disorder</a> type 2B1, mandibuloacral dysplasia, childhood progeria syndrome (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/progeria_syndrome/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Hutchinson-Gilford syndrome</a>) and a subset of Werner syndrome.
