
Endocrine gland: A gland that secretes a substance (a hormone) into the bloodstream. The endocrine glands are "glands of internal secretion." They include the hypothalamus, pituitary gland, pineal gland, thyroid, parathyroid glands, heart (which makes atrial-natriuretic peptide), the stomach and intestines, islets of Langerhans in the pancreas, the adrenal glands, the
kidney (which makes renin, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/erythropoietin/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">erythropoietin</a>, and calcitriol), fat cells (which make leptin). the testes, the ovarian follicle (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estrogens_conjugated-vaginal_cream/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">estrogens</a>) and the corpus luteum in the ovary).
