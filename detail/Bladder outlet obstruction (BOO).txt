
Bladder outlet obstruction (BOO): a blockage at the base of the bladder at the site where urine flows into the urethra to be expelled from the body. Symptoms of bladder outlet syndrome can include feelings of having a full bladder, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/abdominal_pain_causes_remedies_treatment/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">abdominal pain</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/urinary_retention/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">inability to urinate</a> or frequently urinating, a slow flow of urine, problems initiating urination (urinary hesitancy), having to urinate at night, and an intermittent urine stream.
