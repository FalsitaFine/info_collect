
Absinthe: An emerald-green liqueur flavored with extracts of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/wormwood_artemisia_absinthium-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">wormwood</a> plant, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/licorice_glycyrrhiza_glabra-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">licorice</a>, and aromatic flavorings in an alcohol base. Absinthe was manufactured, commercialized, and popularized in France in the late 1700s. It was an extremely addictive drink. Prolonged drinking of absinthe causes convulsions, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blindness/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">blindness</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hallucinations/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">hallucinations</a>, and mental deterioration.
