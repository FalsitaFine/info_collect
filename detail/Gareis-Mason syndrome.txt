
Gareis-Mason syndrome: An 
inherited (genetic) <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5613">syndrome</a> with
characteristic features including (1) neurologically: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20174">mental retardation</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aphasia/article.htm">aphasia</a> (lack of 
speech); (2) limbs: adducted (clasped) thumbs, absent extensor pollicis longus 
and/or brevis muscles to the thumb, shuffling <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3533">gait</a>, and leg <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10974">spasticity</a>; (3) 
growth: small body size; (4) skeleton: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18053">lumbar</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4198">lordosis</a> (sway back). The syndrome is inherited as an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6031">X-linked </a>
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11705"> trait</a> and so affects 
mainly boys. Alternative names include MASA syndrome (MASA stands for mental 
retardation, aphasia, shuffling gait, and adducted thumbs), clasped thumb and 
mental retardation, congenital clasped thumb with mental retardation, and adducted thumb
with mental retardation.
