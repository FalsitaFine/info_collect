
Dermatitis herpetiformis: an autoimmune condition that results in a chronic, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/itch/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">itchy</a> skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rash</a>. Dermatitis herpetiformis is characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/bruises/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">bumps</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blisters/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">blisters</a> occurring primarily on the forearms, knees, elbows, buttocks, back, or scalp. Most people with the condition also have gluten-sensitive enteropathy (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/celiac_disease_gluten_enteropathy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">celiac disease</a>). It is most common in people of Northern European ancestry. The exact cause of dermatitis herpetiformis is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the gluten sensitivity. Treatment is usually effective and consists of a 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/celiac_disease_gluten_free_diet/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">gluten-free diet</a> along with the medication <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dapsone_dds-oral/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">dapsone</a>.
