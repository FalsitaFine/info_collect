
Green fluorescent protein: Abbreviated GFP. A protein that glows green under fluorescent light. Found naturally in the jellyfish Aequorea victoria, GFP fluoresces green when exposed to blue light. It has a sequence of three <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39555" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amino acids</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22833" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">serine</a>-tyrosine-<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22826" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glycine</a>) which is responsible for its fluorescence.
