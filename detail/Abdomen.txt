
Abdomen: The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8124">belly</a>, that part of the body 
that contains all of the structures between the chest and 
the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4824">pelvis</a>. The abdomen is separated anatomically from the chest 
by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9758">diaphragm</a>, the powerful muscle spanning the body cavity below the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4209">lungs</a>. 
The abdomen includes a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/liver_anatomy_and_function/article.htm03">host</a> of organs including the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5560">stomach</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5512"> small intestine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2787">colon</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5253">rectum</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4179">liver</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5531">spleen</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4743">pancreas</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4103"> kidneys</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2312">appendix</a>, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3536"> gallbladder</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2472">bladder</a>. 
The word "abdomen" has a curious story behind it. It comes 
from the Latin "abdodere", to hide. The idea was that whatever was 
eaten was hidden in the abdomen.
