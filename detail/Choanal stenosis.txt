
Choanal stenosis: Abnormal narrowing of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39034" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choana</a>, the passageway from the back of one side of the nose to the throat. The choana is shaped like the opening of a train tunnel in a mountain which then opens into the space called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/pneumonia_facts/article.htm7" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">nasopharynx</a>. There are two <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39038" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choanae</a>. The choanae must be open to permit breathing through the nose. Choanal stenosis can occur as an isolated birth defect in an otherwise normal baby or it may be part of a syndrome. Surgery is needed to repair <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39035" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">choanal</a> stenosis.
