
Nasal flu vaccine: A live attenuated (weakened) influenza virus vaccine that is administered as a nasal spray. The brand name for this vaccine is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/influenza_nasal_vaccine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="PHARM">FluMist</a>. 
The nasal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/influenza/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">flu</a> vaccine is recommended only for healthy people between 5 and 49 years of age. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) recommends this vaccine as an acceptable alternative to the inactivated influenza vaccine for healthy children 5 and over. The AAP also encourages influenza immunization of children 6-23 months of age and household contacts and out-of-home caregivers of children less than 2 years of age.
