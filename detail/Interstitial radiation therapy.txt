
Interstitial radiation therapy: Radiation treatment given by placing radioactive material directly into the target, often a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a>. For example, in treating <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prostate_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">prostate cancer</a>, radioactive seeds are implanted in the prostate gland. The seeds might be titanium-encased pellets containing the radioisotope iodine-125.
Interstitial <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/radiation_therapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">radiation therapy</a> is also called seed implantation or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/brachytherapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">brachytherapy</a> (brachys being Greek for short).
