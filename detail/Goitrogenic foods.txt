
Goitrogenic foods: Goitrogenic are foods that can affect thyroid function by inhibiting synthesis of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5780" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">thyroid hormones</a>, resulting in enlargement of the gland (goiter). Goitrogenic foods include Brussel sprouts, kohlrabi, turnips, rutabaga, radishes, cabbage, kale, and cauliflower.
