
Hypoventilation: The state in which a reduced amount of air enters the alveoli in the lungs, resulting in decreased levels of oxygen and increased levels of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20132" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">carbon dioxide</a> in the blood. Hypoventilation can be due to breathing that is too shallow (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13161" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hypopnea</a>) or too slow (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32268" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bradypnea</a>), or to diminished lung function. The opposite of hypoventilation is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3853" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hyperventilation</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32265" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">overbreathing</a>).
