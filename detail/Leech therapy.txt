
Leech therapy: The use of leeches in medical treatment. Once used as an almost universal cure, leeches were largely abandoned by medicine but in the second half of the 20th century refound a role. That role is largely in plastic and reconstructive <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5603" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">surgery</a>. Leeches can assist, for example, in the reattachment of severed body parts such as a finger, hand, toe, leg, ear, nose or the scalp.
