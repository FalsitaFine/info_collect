
Cuboid bone: The cube-shaped outer bone in the instep of the foot. The cuboid bone has a joint in back that allows it to articulate posteriorly with the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9013" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">calcaneus</a> (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6420" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">heel bone</a>). It also has a joint in the front that permits it to articulate anteriorly with the fourth and fifth <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4365" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">metatarsals</a> (the bones just behind the fourth and fifth toes).
