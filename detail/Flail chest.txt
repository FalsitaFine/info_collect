
Flail chest: A condition that occurs when enough ribs are broken (usually from a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=105198" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">crush injury</a>) to compromise the rigidity of the chest wall. On inspiration, the chest wall moves inward instead of outward, and it does the opposite on expiration.
