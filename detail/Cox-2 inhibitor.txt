
Cox-2 inhibitor: An antiinflammatory drug that selectively blocks the cox-2 enzyme. Blocking this enzyme impedes the production of the chemical messengers that cause the pain and swelling of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">arthritis</a> inflammation. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cox-2_inhibitors/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">Cox-2 inhibitors</a> do not pose as great a risk of injuring the stomach or intestines as drugs that block cox-1. An example of a cox-2 inhibitor is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/celecoxib/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">celecoxib</a> (brand name: Celebrex).
