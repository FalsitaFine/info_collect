
Autoimmune <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/thyroid_disorders/article.htm">thyroid disease</a>: Disease of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5778">thyroid gland</a> due to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18985">autoimmunity</a> in which the patient's <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3907">immune system</a> attacks and damages their thyroid. Graves disease and Hashimoto <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/thyroiditis/article.htm">thyroiditis</a> are forms of autoimmune <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/thyroid_disorders/article.htm">thyroid disease</a>. In these diseases, thyroid-reactive T cells are formed that infiltrate the thyroid gland. Both disorders are common with a prevalence of about 1% in the US.
