
Amygdaloid nucleus: A small oval structure in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20829" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">temporal lobe</a>  of the brain that is closely connected to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3866" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hypothalamus</a>, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3757" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hippocampus</a>, and the cingulate gyrus. The amygdaloid nucleus is part of the olefactory and limbic systems and plays a role in the sense of smell, motivation, and emotional behavior.
It was named the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39192" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amygdaloid</a> nucleus because anatomists thought it was shaped like an almond. From the Greek amydale (almond) + the Greek eidos (like). Also called the amygdala, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39205" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amygdaloid body</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39206" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amygdaloid complex</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39209" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">corpus amygdaloideum</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39210" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">nucleus amygdalae</a>.
