
Capsaicin: Capsaicin is an active component of chili peppers that is used to relieve <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/muscle_pain/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">muscle pain</a>, joint pain, and nerve pain associated with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/osteoarthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">osteoarthritis</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rheumatoid_arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rheumatoid arthritis</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetic_neuropathy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetic neuropathy</a>.  It is available in topical cream, ointment, stick, pad, gel, liquid, and lotion.  It appears to work by reducing a chemical, substance P, found at nerve endings involved in transmitting pain signals to the brain.
