
AIH (artificial insemination by husband): A procedure in which a fine
catheter (tube) is inserted through the cervix (the natural opening of the
uterus) into the uterus (the womb) to deposit a sperm sample from the
woman's mate directly into the uterus. The purpose of this procedure is to
achieve fertilization and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a>. AIH is distinguished from artificial
insemination by donor (AID) in which the donor is a man other than the
woman's mate. AIH is also known as homologous insemination.
