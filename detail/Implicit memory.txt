
Implicit memory: Memory characterized by a lack of conscious awareness in the act of recollection. By contrast, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33197" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">explicit memory</a> requires conscious recollection of previous experience. Implicit memory may survive largely unimpaired at the same time as a person's powers of explicit memory decline with age or are devastated in Alzheimer disease.
