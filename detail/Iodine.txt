
Iodine: An element in the diet that is essential for the manufacture of hormones by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5778" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">thyroid gland</a>. The thyroid gland uses iodine to make thyroxine (T4), which has four iodine molecules attached to its structure, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5851" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">triiodothyronine</a> (T3), which has three iodine molecules attached. Iodine is found in seafood, bread, iodized salt, and seaweed.
