
Headache, tension: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm">headache</a> caused by 
contracting of the muscles in the back of the neck, on the 
scalp, and sometimes in the jaw. Tension headaches are 
usually related to stress. Treatment is via stress 
reduction, massage, and analgesic medication, such as 
aspirin (in adults only), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/acetaminophen/article.htm">acetaminophen</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ibuprofen/article.htm">ibuprofen</a>, or 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/naproxen/article.htm"> naproxen</a> sodium. Analgesics containing caffeine may be 
stronger. For chronic tension headaches, some doctors 
recommend tricyclic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/antidepressants/article.htm">antidepressants</a>, such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/amitriptyline/article.htm">amitriptyline</a>, 
which work to defuse stress and to lessen pain. Also called 
muscle tension headache, stress headache.
