
Endothelium: A layer of flat cells lining the closed
internal spaces of the body such as the inside of blood vessels and lymphatic vessels (that convey the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4212">lymph</a>, a milky fluid) and the heart. 
By contrast, the outside layer of cells that
covers all the free, open surfaces of the body including the skin, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10681">mucous</a> membranes that communicate with the outside of the body is called the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3293">epithelium</a>.
The word "endothelium" is derived from the Greek "endon," within + G. "thele," nipple.
