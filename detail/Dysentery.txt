
Dysentery: Inflammation of the intestine, with pain, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a>, bloody stools, and often a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a> above 38.3'C (101'F). The causes of dysentery include bacteria (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/shigella_infection/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Shigella</a>), protozoa (such as amebae), parasitic worms (such as schistosomes), and viruses. Dysentery can be fatal because it can cause severe <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dehydration/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">dehydration</a>. Treatment includes rapid rehydration, sometimes via IV, and medication.
