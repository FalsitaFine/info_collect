
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/neutropenia/article.htm">Neutropenia</a>: Not enough neutrophils, neutrophils being a type of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6017">white blood cell</a> (specifically a form of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8780">granulocyte</a>) filled with neutrally-staining granules, tiny sacs of enzymes that help the cell to kill and digest microorganisms it has engulfed by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=17895">phagocytosis</a>. The mature neutrophil has a segmented <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4597">nucleus</a> (it is called a seg or poly) while the immature neutrophil has band-shape nucleus (it is called a band). The neutrophil has a lifespan of about 3 days.
