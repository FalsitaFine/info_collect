
Acid, amino: One of the 20 building blocks of protein. The sequence of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39555" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amino acids</a> in a protein and, hence, the function of that protein are determined by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3574" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">genetic code</a> in the DNA.
Amino acids are molecules that (in technical terms) contain a basic amino (NH2) group, an acidic carboxyl (COOH) group and a side chain attached to an alpha carbon atom.
The 20 amino acids are alanine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7006" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">arginine</a>, asparagine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22822" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">aspartic acid</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22823" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cysteine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22824" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glutamic acid</a>, glutamine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22826" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glycine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22827" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">histidine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22828" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">isoleucine</a>, leucine, lysine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22831" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">methionine</a>, phenylalanine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22832" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">proline</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22833" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">serine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22834" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">threonine</a>, tryptophan, tyrosine, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22837" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">valine</a>.  The term
"amino acids" dates to the middle of the 19<sup>th</sup> century. The idea that
amino acids are "Bausteine" (building stones) came from the Nobel Prize winning
German biochemist Albrecht Kossel (1853-1927).
