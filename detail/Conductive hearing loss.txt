
Conductive hearing loss: a type of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/deafness/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hearing loss</a> resulting when sound is not conducted effectively through the ear canal to the eardrum and the small bones of the middle ear. This is in contrast to sensorineural <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hearing_loss/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">hearing loss</a>, which is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/deafness/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">hearing</a> loss due to damage to the inner ear or the nerve that carries sound signals to the `brain. Sometimes, both kinds of hearing loss are present at the same time.
