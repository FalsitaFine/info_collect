
Microcephaly: An abnormally small head due to failure of brain growth. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/microcephaly/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Microcephaly</a> is an ominous sign because it is almost always associated with developmental delay and mental retardation. Many factors can impair the growth of the brain, including intrauterine infections (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/german_measles/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rubella</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cytomegalovirus_cmv/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cytomegalovirus</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/toxoplasmosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">toxoplasmosis</a>), intrauterine chemical exposure (such as in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fetal_alcohol_syndrome_fas/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fetal alcohol syndrome</a>), excessive radiation exposure (as in an atomic bomb explosion), chromosome abnormalities (such as trisomy 13 and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/trisomy_18_edwards_syndrome/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">trisomy 18</a>), and genetic syndromes (such as Fanconi syndrome and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/williams_syndrome/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Williams syndrome</a>). Recently, infection with the<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/zika_virus_causes_symptoms_treatment_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide"> Zika virus</a> during <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy_planning_preparing_for_pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a> has been associated with microcephaly in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/babies_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">newborn</a>. The opposite of microcephaly is macrocephaly.
