
Acromion: The projection of the scapula (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8207" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">shoulder blade</a>) that forms the point of the shoulder. The acromion is part of the scapula. It protrudes laterally (away from the midline) and is triangular in shape. The top of the shoulder is acromial. The word "acromion" comes from the Greek "akron", peak + "omos", shoulder = the peak of the shoulder.
