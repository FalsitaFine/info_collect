
Genome: All the genetic information possessed by any organism (for example, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3818" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">human genome</a>, the elephant genome, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13889" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mouse genome</a>, the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13872" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">yeast genome</a>, and the genome of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2416" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bacterium</a>). Humans and many other higher animals actually have two genomes'a chromosomal genome and a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4401" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mitochondrial genome</a>'that together make up their genome.
