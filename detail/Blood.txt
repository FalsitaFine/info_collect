
Blood: The familiar red fluid in the body that contains <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6017">white</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15489">red blood cells</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4941">platelets</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15380">proteins</a>, and other elements. The blood is transported throughout the body by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2738">circulatory system</a>. Blood functions in two directions: arterial and venous. Arterial blood is the means by which <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10690">oxygen</a> and nutrients are transported to tissues while venous blood is the means by which <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20132">carbon dioxide</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18074">metabolic</a> by-products are transported to the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4209">lungs</a> and kidneys, respectively, for removal from the body.
