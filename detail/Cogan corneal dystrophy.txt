
Cogan corneal dystrophy: A disorder in which the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7248" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cornea</a> shows grayish fingerprint lines, geographic map-like lines, and dots (or microcysts). These lines and dots can be seen on examination with a slit-lamp, which focuses a high-intensity light beam through a slit while the examiner uses a magnifying scope to look at the front of the eye where the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=20649" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epithelial</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39157" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">basement membrane</a> is seen as abnormal. The disorder is usually without symptoms. However, about 1 patient in 10 has recurrent erosion of the cornea that generally begins after age 30. Also known as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7286" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">epithelial basement corneal dystrophy</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7284" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">map-dot-fingerprint type corneal dystrophy</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7283" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">microcystic corneal dystrophy</a>.
