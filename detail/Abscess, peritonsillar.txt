
Abscess, peritonsillar: An abscess behind the tonsils that pushes one of the tonsils toward the uvula (the prominent soft tissue dangling from the back of the palate in the back of the mouth). A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7449" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">peritonsillar abscess</a> is generally very painful and associated with difficulty opening the mouth. If a peritonsillar abscess is untreated, the infection can spread deep into the neck, causing <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8541" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">airway obstruction</a> and other life-threatening complications.
