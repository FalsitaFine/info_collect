
Affordable Care Act (ACA): the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/affordable_care_act_aca_or_obamacare/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ACA</a> is the US health care law that was enacted in March 2010. The law consists of two pieces of legislation: the 
<a href="http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/BILLS-111hr3590enr/pdf/BILLS-111hr3590enr.pdf" onclick="popupWarning();" target="_blank">Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act</a> (PPACA) and the  
<a href="http://www.dpc.senate.gov/healthreformbill/healthbill61.pdf" onclick="popupWarning();" target="_blank">Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act</a> (HCERA). According to the American Public Health Association, the APA 
"contains numerous provisions that will expand health coverage to 30 million 
Americans, increase benefits and lower costs for consumers, provide new funding 
for public health and prevention, bolster our health care and public health 
workforce and infrastructure, foster innovation and quality in our system, and 
more."
