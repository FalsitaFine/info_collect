
Iso-: Equal, like, or similar. Iso- is much used in medicine and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13754" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">science</a> as, for example, in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4058" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">isotonic solution</a> (a solution that has the same salt concentration as cells and blood), isotope (a form of a chemical element with the same atomic number as another element but having a different atomic mass), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=25444" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">isocellular</a> (composed of cells of the same size and type), and isochromosome (a chromosome with two identical equal arms). The opposite of iso- is aniso-.
Iso- is derived from the Greek "isos" meaning "equal or like."
