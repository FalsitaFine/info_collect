
Epstein-Barr virus: A virus in the herpes family that is best known as the cause of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/infectious_mononucleosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">infectious mononucleosis</a> (also called mono and glandular <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/aches_pain_fever/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fever</a>). Abbreviated EBV. EBV infection is characterized by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fatigue</a> and general malaise. Infection with EBV is common and is normally temporary and minor. However, in some individuals EBV can trigger chronic illness, including immune and lymphoproliferative syndromes. It is a particular danger to people with compromised immune systems, including those with AIDS. Treatment is with antiviral medication and rest. Also known as human herpesvirus 4 (HHV-4).
