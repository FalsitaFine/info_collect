
Intramuscular (IM): An intramuscular (IM) medication is given by needle into the muscle. This is as opposed to a medication that is given by a needle, for example, into the skin (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4009" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">intradermal</a>) or just below the skin (subcutaneous) or into a vein
