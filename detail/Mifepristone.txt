
Mifepristone: The French abortion pill, also known as RU-486. RU-486 has been used in combination with another drug called <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/misoprostol/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">misoprostol</a>, to terminate <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a> at an early stage. It is used with women who are no more than 7 weeks pregnant. The drug has 95% effectiveness. About 5% of women using RU-486 in early pregnancies need additional medical intervention because of incomplete abortions. (doctors often intervene at higher rates in cases of natural miscarriages). The side effects  with RU-486 are similar to those of a natural <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/miscarriage_causes_and_symptoms/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">miscarriage</a>.
