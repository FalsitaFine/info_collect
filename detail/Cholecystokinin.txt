
Cholecystokinin: Abbreviated CCK. A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7766" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">polypeptide</a> hormone that stimulates the contraction of the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3536" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">gallbladder</a> with release of bile and the secretion of pancreatic enzymes into the small intestine. CCK is secreted by cells lining the upper intestine and by the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3866" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">hypothalamus</a>. Hypothalamic cholecystokinin is a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9973" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">neurotransmitter</a>. Called also <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=24964" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">pancreozymin</a>.
