
Microdeletion: Loss of a tiny piece that may be too small to be seen readily through a microscope'from a chromosome. Microdeletions can be detected via high-resolution <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15921" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chromosome banding</a>, molecular chromosome analysis (with FISH), or DNA analysis. Disorders caused by microdeletions include Angelman, DiGeorge, Prader-Willi, and Williams syndromes.
