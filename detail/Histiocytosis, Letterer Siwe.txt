
Histiocytosis, Letterer Siwe: A severe disease in which histiocytes start to multiply and attack the tissues or organs of the patient starting in infancy with a scaly, sometimes itchy <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rash</a> on the scalp, ears, abdomen, and creases of the neck and face. Raw skin may provide a portal of entry for germs, leading to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sepsis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">sepsis</a> (bloodstream infection). There are often draining ears, swollen glands, enlargement of the liver and spleen, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/liver_disease/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">liver disease</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/anemia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">anemia</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/neutropenia/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">neutropenia</a> (low white blood cells). Loss of appetite, irritability, failure to thrive, and respiratory symptoms may occur. Because of their appearance, these children may be misdiagnosed as abused. Letterer-Siwe disease is the most severe form of Langerhans cell histiocytosis.
