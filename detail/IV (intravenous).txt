
IV (intravenous): Within a vein. IV is the abbreviation for 
"intravenous." 
The word "intravenous" is quite properly an adjective. In this 
guise, it entered the English language around 1849. It means, 
according to Merriam Webster's Collegiate Dictionary, "situated, 
performed, or occurring within or entering by way of a vein."
Some examples of the use of "intravenous" as an adjective:<ul>
<li>Intravenous antibiotics refer to the administration 
  into the venous circulation of a solution containing antibiotics; 
  
<li>Intravenous immunoglobulin is a sterile solution of 
  concentrated antibodies extracted from healthy people given straight into a 
  vein; 
  
<li>An intravenous injection is just that: the giving of something 
such as drugs into a vein through a syringe.</li></li></li></ul>
The accompanying adverb is "intravenously", as in: "The <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glucose/article.htm">glucose</a> 
was 
given intravenously."
The word "intravenous" as a noun refers to an intravenous 
fluid drip, a solution (usually a balanced electrolyte solution) 
administered directly into the venous circulation. Also called a 
drip.
