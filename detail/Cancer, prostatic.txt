
Cancer, prostatic: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Cancer</a> of the gland (prostate) that
produces some of the components of semen fluid. The second leading cause of death of males
in the U.S. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prostate_cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Prostate cancer</a> is often first detected as a hard nodule during a routine
rectal examination. The PSA blood test is a screening test for prostate cancer. Diagnosis
of prostate cancer is established when cancer cells are identified in prostate tissue
obtained by a biopsy. In some patients, prostate cancer is life threatening. In many
others, prostate cancer can exist for years without causing any health problems. Treatment
options for prostate cancer include observation, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/radiation_therapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">radiation therapy</a>, surgery, hormonal
therapy, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chemotherapy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">chemotherapy</a>.
