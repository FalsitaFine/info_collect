
Knee bursitis: A bursa is a fluid-filled sac that
functions as a gliding surface to reduce friction between moving tissues of the body. There are three major bursae of the knee. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/acute_and_chronic_bursitis/article.htm">Bursitis</a> is usually not infectious, but the bursa can become infected. Treatment of non-infectious bursitis includes rest, ice, and medications for inflammation and pain. Infectious bursitis is treated with antibiotics, aspiration, and surgery.
