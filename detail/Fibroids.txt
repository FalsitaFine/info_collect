
Fibroids: Fibroids are common, benign tumors of smooth muscle in the uterus (womb). <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/uterine_fibroids/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Uterine fibroids</a> are the most common reason for performing a
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hysterectomy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc"> hysterectomy</a>. Fibroids do not produce symptoms in all women, but may lead to prolonged or heavy menstrual  bleeding, pelvic pressure or pain, and rarely to  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/infertility/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">infertility</a>. In addition to hysterectomy, less invasive surgical procedures have been employed to remove uterine fibroids. Fibroids can be detected by radiologic testing, such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ultrasound/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">ultrasound</a>, CAT scan, or magnetic resonance imaging  (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/mri_scan/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">MRI</a>).
