
Dermatitis, stasis: A skin irritation on the lower legs, generally related to circulatory problems.
Stasis refers to a stoppage or slowdown in the flow of blood (or other body fluid such as lymph). A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9607" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">stasis ulcer</a> is an ulcer (a crater) that develops in an area in which the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2735" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">circulation</a> is sluggish and the venous return (the return of venous blood toward the heart) is poor. A common location for stasis ulcers is on the ankle. "Stasis" is a Greek word meaning "the posture for standing" and is derived from the Greek "histemi" meaning "to make stand still."
