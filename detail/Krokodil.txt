
Krokodil: a street drug from Russia that has been used as a substitute for <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3729" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">heroin</a>, according to news reports. It is reported to cause scaling, sores, and a rough appearance to the skin. The drug is reported to be synthesized from materials that one can obtain from pharmacies and home improvement stores; the finished product is injected intravenously. Some of the chemicals used to prepare the drug have apparently caused significant damage to the skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8265" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">subcutaneous</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3394" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">fat</a>.
