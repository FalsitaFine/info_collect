
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3266" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Enzyme</a>: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15380" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Proteins</a> that speeds up the rate of a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6760" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">chemical reaction</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> a living organism. An enzyme acts as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6758" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">catalyst</a> for specific chemical reactions, converting a specific set of reactants (called substrates) into specific products. Without <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15392" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">enzymes</a>, life as we know it would not exist.
