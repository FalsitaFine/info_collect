
Milligray: A unit of absorbed <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11946">radiation</a> equal to 0.001 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32310">gray</a>. (A gray is the dose of one joule of energy absorbed per kilogram of matter, or 100 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11944">rad</a>.) Because the gray is such a large unit, many radiation measurements are made in milligrays. In particular, exposures from by <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6032">X-ray</a> equipment are typically expressed in the milligray range. The abbreviation for milligray is <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=32309">mGy</a>.
