
McCune-Albright syndrome: A <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3573">genetic</a> disorder of bones, skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=9681">pigmentation</a> and hormonal problems with premature sexual <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2964">development</a>. Also called Albright syndrome or polyostotic fibrous <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3142">dysplasia</a>.  In the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5613">syndrome</a>, there is bone disease with fractures and deformity of the legs, arms and skull; <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/stroke_symptoms_and_treatment/article.htm9">pigment</a> patches of the skin; and endocrine (hormonal) disease with early <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5118">puberty</a> (early menstrual bleeding, development of breasts and pubic hair) and an increased rate of growth. The McCune-Albright syndrome is usually caused by mosaicism for a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4471">mutation</a> in a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3560">gene</a> called GNAS1 (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3639">Guanine</a> <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4596">Nucleotide</a> binding <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6554">protein</a>, Alpha Stimulating activity <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7766">polypeptide</a> 1).
