
Atherosclerosis: A process of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10697">progressive</a> thickening and hardening of the walls of medium-sized and large arteries as a result of fat deposits on their inner lining. 
Risk factors for atherosclerosis include high levels of "bad" <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2710">cholesterol</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3756">high blood pressure</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3846">hypertension</a>), smoking, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11148">diabetes</a> and a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3573">genetic</a> family history of atherosclerotic disease.
Atherosclerosis is responsible for much <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10267">coronary artery disease</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6594">angina</a> and 
heart attacks) and many strokes.
