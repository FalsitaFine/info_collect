
Frey syndrome: Sweating on one side of the forehead, face, scalp, and neck that occurs soon after ingestion of food, as a result of damage to a nerve that goes to the large saliva gland in the cheek (the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4785" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">parotid gland</a>). The symptoms are usually mild, but more severe cases may require medical treatment.
