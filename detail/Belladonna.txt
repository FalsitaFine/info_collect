
Belladonna: An herbaceous plant bearing the scientific name <i>Atropa belladonna</i> or 
<i>Atropa bella-donna</i>, also known as deadly nightshade. The leaves and berries of the 
plant are highly toxic and can lead to hallucinations and delirium when 
ingested. Eating just a few berries from the plant can be fatal in children. The 
belladonna plant has a long history of use by humans for medicines, cosmetics, 
and poisons. It is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa but has been 
naturalized in parts of North America.
