
Attention deficit disorder (ADD): (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ADD</a>) a developmental disorder characterized by symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/inattention/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">inattention</a> (such as distractibility, disorganization, or forgetfulness) or by symptoms of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/hyperactivity/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">hyperactivity</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/impulsivity/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">impulsivity</a> (such as fidgeting, speaking out of turn, or restlessness). Also referred to as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">attention deficit</a>-hyperactivity disorder (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_in_children/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">ADHD</a>). <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_symptoms_and_signs/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">ADD</a> or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/adhd_children_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">ADHD</a> is not caused by an underlying physical or mental disorder.
