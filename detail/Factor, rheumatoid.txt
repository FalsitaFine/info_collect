
Factor, rheumatoid: An antibody that is measurable in the blood. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rheumatoid_factor/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">Rheumatoid factor</a> is commonly used as a blood test for the diagnosis of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rheumatoid_arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rheumatoid arthritis</a>. Rheumatoid factor is present in about 80 percent of adults (and a much lower proportion of children) with rheumatoid <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">arthritis</a>. It is also present in patients with other connective tissue diseases, such as systemic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/systemic_lupus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">lupus</a> erythematosus and Sj'gren's syndrome, and in some with infectious diseases, including infectious hepatitis.
