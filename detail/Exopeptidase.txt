
Exopeptidase: An enzyme that catalyzes the cleavage of the terminal (last) or next-to-last peptide bond from a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7766" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">polypeptide</a> or protein, releasing a single amino acid or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39554" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">dipeptide</a>. By contrast, an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39552" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">endopeptidase</a> catalyzes the cleavage of then internal peptide bonds within a polypeptide or protein.
