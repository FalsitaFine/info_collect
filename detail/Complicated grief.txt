
Complicated grief: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/loss_grief_and_bereavement/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Grief</a> that is complicated by adjustment disorders (especially <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/depression/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">depressed</a> and anxious mood or disturbed emotions and behavior), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/depression_overview_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">major depression</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/drug_abuse/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">substance abuse</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/generalized_anxiety_disorder_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">post-traumatic stress disorder</a> are  Complicated <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/grief_bereavement_mourning_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">grief</a> is identified by the extended length of time of the symptoms, the interference in normal function caused by the symptoms, or by the intensity of the symptoms (for example, intense <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/suicide/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">suicidal thoughts</a> or acts).

Complicated grief may appear as a complete absence of grief and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/grief_bereavement_mourning_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">mourning</a>, an ongoing inability to experience normal grief reactions, delayed grief, conflicted grief, or chronic grief. Factors that contribute to the chance that one may experience complicated grief include the suddenness of the death, the gender of the person in mourning, and the relationship to the deceased (for example, an intense, extremely close, or very contradictory relationship). Grief reactions that turn into major <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/depression/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">depression</a> should be treated with both drug and psychological therapy. One who avoids any reminders of the person who died, who constantly thinks or dreams about the person who died, and who gets scared and panics easily at any reminders of the person who died may be suffering from <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/generalized_anxiety_disorder_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">post-traumatic stress</a> disorder. Substance abuse may occur, frequently in an attempt to avoid painful feelings about the loss and symptoms (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/causes_of_fatigue_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">sleeplessness</a>), and can also be treated with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/drugs_what_you_should_know_about_your_drugs/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">drugs</a> and psychological therapy.
Also called unresolved grief.
