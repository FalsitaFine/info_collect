
Cod liver oil: An oil extracted from the liver of the cod. Cod liver oil was once given religiously to children every day as a rich  source of vitamins A and D. It was also used to treat children with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rickets/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rickets</a>, a bone disease due to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/vitamin_d_deficiency/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">vitamin D deficiency</a>.  Cod liver oil is now known to be rich in omega-3 fatty acids. Perhaps because of this, cod liver oil helps slow the destruction of joint cartilage in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/osteoarthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">osteoarthritis</a>.
Anyone who was ever given cod liver oil, even years ago, can still remember its distasteful odor and foul taste. It reportedly comes in more palatable forms today.
