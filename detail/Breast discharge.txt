
Breast discharge: The spontaneous flow of fluid from the nipple at any time other than during nursing. This can be due to an unrecognized <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pregnancy/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pregnancy</a>, trauma, surgery, overexercise, or certain drugs. It can also be due to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cirrhosis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cirrhosis</a> of the liver, false pregnancy (pseudocyesis), <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/kidney_failure/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">kidney failure</a>, disorders of the spinal cord, or a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/prolactinoma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">prolactinoma</a>, a benign pituitary <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tumor_grade/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">tumor</a> that secretes the hormone prolactin stimulating milk production.
