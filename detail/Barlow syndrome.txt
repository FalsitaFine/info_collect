
Barlow syndrome: Barlow syndrome is
 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/mitral_valve_prolapse/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">mitral valve prolapse</a> (also known as "click murmur syndrome"), the most common
heart valve abnormality, affecting 5-10% of the world population. Most patients have no
symptoms and require no treatment. However, the condition can be associated with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/fatigue/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">fatigue </a>
and/or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/palpitations_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">palpitations</a>. The mitral valve prolapse can often be detected by a doctor during
examination of the heart and can be confirmed with an <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/echocardiogram/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">echocardiogram</a>. Patients are usually
given antibiotics prior to any procedure which might introduce bacteria into the
bloodstream, including dental work and minor surgery.
