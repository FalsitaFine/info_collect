
Klippel-Feil sequence: Short neck, low hairline at the nape of the neck and limited movement of the head. Klippel-Feil sequence is due to a defect in the early development of the spinal column in the neck, resulting in fusion of the  <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8761" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cervical vertebrae</a>. The condition is named for the French neurologists Maurice Klippel and Andre Feil who in 1912 reported "A case of absence of the cervical vertebrae with the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=18064" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">thoracic cage</a> going up to the base of the skull."
Klippel-Feil sequence is now the preferred name for the condition.
