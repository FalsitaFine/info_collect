
Estrogen-associated blood clots: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blood_clots/article.htm">Blood clots</a> are occasional but serious side effects of estrogen therapy. They are dose-related, that is, they occur more frequently with higher doses of estrogen.
Estrogen therapy preparations (all of which carry this risk) include:<ul>
<li>esterified <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/conjugated_equine_estrogens_and_medroxyprogesteron/article.htm">estrogens</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/esterified_estrogens_and_methyltestosterone/article.htm">esterified estrogens and methyltestosterone</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estradiol/article.htm">estradiol</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/conjugated_equine_estrogens_and_medroxyprogesteron/article.htm">estrogens</a> (conjugated) and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/medroxyprogesterone/article.htm">medroxyprogesterone</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estrogens_conjugated/article.htm">estrogens conjugated</a></li>
</ul>
The brand names of the estrogen therapy preparations carrying this risk include:<ul>
<li>CLIMARA (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estradiol/article.htm">estradiol</a>)</li>
<li>ESTRACE (estradiol)</li>
<li>ESTRADERM (estradiol)</li>
<li>ESTRATAB (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/esterified_estrogens/article.htm">esterified estrogens</a>)</li>
<li>ESTRATEST (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/esterified_estrogens_and_methyltestosterone/article.htm">esterified estrogens and methyltestosterone</a> \)</li>
<li>MENEST (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/esterified_estrogens/article.htm">esterified estrogens</a>)</li>
<li>OGEN (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estropipate/article.htm">estropipate</a>)</li>
<li>PREMARIN (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/estrogens_conjugated/article.htm">estrogens conjugated</a>)</li>
<li>PREMPHASE (estrogens conjugated and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/medroxyprogesterone/article.htm">medroxyprogesterone</a>)</li>
<li>PREMPRO (estrogens conjugated and medroxyprogesterone)</li>
</ul>
Cigarette smokers on estrogen therapy are at a higher risk than non-smokers for blood clots. Therefore, patients requiring estrogen therapy are strongly encouraged to quit <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/smoking_and_quitting_smoking/article.htm">smoking</a>.
