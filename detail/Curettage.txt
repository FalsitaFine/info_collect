
Curettage: Removal of tissue with a curette from the wall of a cavity or another surface. For example, curettage may be done to remove <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/skin_cancer_overview/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">skin cancer</a>. After a local anesthetic numbs the area, the skin <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a> is scooped out with a curette. Curettage may also be done in the uterus; <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dilation_and_curettage/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="proc">dilation and curettage</a> (D&amp;C) refers to the dilation (widening) of the cervical canal to permit curettage of the endometrium, the inner lining of the uterus.
