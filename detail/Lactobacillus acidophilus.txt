
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6204" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Lactobacillus acidophilus</a>: This <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=13954" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">bacteria</a> is part of the normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12133" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">flora</a> of the human and animal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=33422" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">mouth</a>, intestinal tract, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5951" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">vagina</a>. As normal flora, it helps to prevent <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4707" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">overgrowth</a> of potentially harmful bacteria by maintaining an acidic <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/liver_anatomy_and_function/article.htm04" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">environment</a>. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8697" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Acidophilus</a> and some related bacteria are considered to be "<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11901" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">probiotic</a>" because they may help the body maintain or restore its normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10760" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">balance</a> of helpful bacteria. The use of antibiotics can decimate the normal flora <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3950" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">in</a> the body. Some healthcare professionals recommend <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=97587" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">probiotics</a> to counter the side effect of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8121" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">antibiotic</a> use. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=12126" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">L</a>. acidophilus is found in milk and fermented products like <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6062" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">yogurt</a> with "active cultures" and in the form of supplements.
