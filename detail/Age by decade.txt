
Age by decade: A term designating someone's age by decade. For example, a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22679" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">septuagenarian</a> refers to someone in his or her seventies (age 70 to 79). The prefix in such terms is always from the Latin. For example, the Latin septuageni = seventy. <ul>
<li>Denarian: Someone age 10 to 19.
<li>Vicenarian: Someone in his or her twenties.
<li>Tricenarian: Someone in his or her thirties.
<li>Quadragenarian: Someone in his or her forties.
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22681" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Quinquagenarian</a>: Someone in his or her fifties.
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22680" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Sexagenarian</a>: Someone in his or her sixties.
<li>Septuagenarian: Someone in his or her seventies.
<li>Octogenarian: Someone in his or her eighties.
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22677" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Nonagenarian</a>: Someone in his or her nineties.
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22676" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Centenarian</a>: Someone 100 or more.
<li><a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22675" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Supercentenarian</a>: Someone 110 years old or more (no upper limit).</li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></li></ul>
The terms denarian, vicenarian, tricenarian, and quadragenarian are not in common usage. The term supercentenarian was coined in 1991.
