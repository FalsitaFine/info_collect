
Hemostasis: The stoppage of bleeding or hemorrhage. Also, the stoppage of blood flow through a blood vessel or organ of the body. Hemostasis is the arrest of bleeding, whether it be by normal <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=5964" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">vasoconstriction</a> (the vessel walls closing temporarily), by an abnormal obstruction (such as a plaque) or by coagulation or surgical means (such as ligation). The term comes from the Greek roots <i>heme</i>, blood + <i>stasis</i>, halt = halt of the blood.
