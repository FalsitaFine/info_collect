
Electrocochleography: A test that measures the electrical potentials generated in the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7210" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">inner ear</a> in response to stimulation by sound. Electrocochleography may be done, for example, to confirm the diagnosis of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8824" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Meniere disease</a>. Abbreviated <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=21719" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ECochG</a> (or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=21720" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">ECoG</a>).
