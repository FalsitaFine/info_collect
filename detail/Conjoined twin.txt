
Conjoined twin: Identical (monozygotic) twins that did not separate fully from one another but are still partially united. Due to the incomplete division of one fertilized ovum. <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=26738" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">Conjoined twins</a> are popularly known as Siamese twins
after Chang and Eng (1811-1874), the celebrated conjoined Chinese twins born in Siam (Thailand).  Conjoin comes via French from the Latin conjungere, from com- + jungere, to join = to join or yoke together.
