
Mycoplasma genitalium:  a type of bacteria that can cause a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stds_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">sexually transmitted disease</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/std_quiz/quiz.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="quiz">STD</a>), also known as a <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stds_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">sexually transmitted infection</a> (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/stds_pictures_slideshow/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="slide">STI</a>). It is estimated that up to 1% of all adults may be infected with <i>M. genitalium</i>. The infection does not always cause symptoms, but when symptoms do occur, they resemble those of other <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sexually_transmitted_diseases_stds_in_women/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">STDs</a> like <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/is_chlamydia_contagious/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">chlamydia</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/gonorrhea_in_women/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">gonorrhea</a>. In men, a burning sensation when urinating and discharge from the penis are the most common symptoms. Most infected women do not have symptoms, but some may develop <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/vaginal_discharge/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">vaginal discharge</a>, abnormal bleeding (such as bleeding between periods or after sex), and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/pelvic_pain_in_women_and_men/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">pelvic pain</a>.
