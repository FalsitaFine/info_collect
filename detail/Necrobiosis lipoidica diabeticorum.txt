
Necrobiosis lipoidica diabeticorum: A dull red raised area on the skin that evolves into a shiny   scar with a violet border, most often on the shin. There is telangiectasia with   blood vessels easily visible under the skin. The area be itchy and painful and   crack open. Occurs in 0.3% of patients with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a>.  Also seen in <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rheumatoid_arthritis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">rheumatoid arthritis</a>.  Treatment is variable, and may include support stockings, leg elevation.  Medication studied for NLD includes topical and intralesional steroids, topical <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/tacrolimus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">tacrolimus</a>, cyclosporine, bovine collagen, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/ticlopidine/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="pharm">ticlopidine</a>, nicotinamide, clofazimine, and intralesional heparin.  Ultraviolet Light A (UVA) therapy has also been studied for NLD.
