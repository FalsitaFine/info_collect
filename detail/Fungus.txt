
Fungus: A single-celled or multicellular organism. Fungi can be true pathogens (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=3764">histoplasmosis</a> and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=15206">coccidioidomycosis</a>) that cause infections in healthy persons  or they can be opportunistic pathogens (such as <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10948">aspergillosis</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11073">candidiasis</a>, and cryptococcosis) that cause infections in immunocompromised persons (including cancer patients, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6290">transplant</a> recipients, and persons with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2181">AIDS</a>). An example of a common fungus is the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=6051">yeast</a> organism which causes <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=10313">thrush</a> and diaper <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/rash/article.htm">rash</a> (diaper <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2951">dermatitis</a>). Fungi are also used for the development of antibiotics, antitoxins, and other drugs used to control various human diseases.
