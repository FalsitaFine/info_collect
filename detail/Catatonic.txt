
Catatonic: 1. Characterized by marked <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=4439">motor</a> abnormalities including immobility (<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11104">catalepsy</a> or stupor), excessive motor activity (purposeless agitation), extreme negativism, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7870">mutism</a>, posturing or stereotyped movements, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/human_immunodeficiency_virus_hiv/article.htm15">echolalia</a>, and/or <a href="https://www.medicinenet.comhttps://www.medicinenet.com/human_immunodeficiency_virus_hiv/article.htm16">echopraxia</a>.<br>
2. A person with catatonia or catatonic schizophrenia.</br>
