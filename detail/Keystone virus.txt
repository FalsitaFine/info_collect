
Keystone virus: a virus first isolated in 1964 from mosquitoes in Keystone, Florida that has recently been described as being isolated from a human host. The virus is known to infect animal populations in coastal regions from Texas to the Chesapeake Bay and is carried by the Aedes atlanticus mosquito. Despite evidence that humans make antibodies to the virus, it has never been isolated from a human host until now. In June 2018 a report identified that the virus was identified from laboratory samples taken from a teenager who became sick in Florida during the 2016 <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/zika_virus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">Zika virus</a> outbreak.
