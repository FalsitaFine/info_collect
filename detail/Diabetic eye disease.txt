
Diabetic eye disease: 1. A disease of the small blood vessels of the retina of the eye in people with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diabetes_mellitus/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diabetes</a>. The vessels swell and leak liquid into the retina, blurring the vision and sometimes leading to <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/blindness/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">blindness</a>. Also called diabetic retinopathy. 2. Any eye disease to which diabetes predisposes including not only diabetic retinopathy but also <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cataracts/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cataracts</a> (clouding of the lens) and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/glaucoma/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">glaucoma</a> (increased fluid pressure inside the eye that can lead to optic nerve damage and loss of vision). It is recommended that all diabetics have a dilated eye examination at least once a year.
