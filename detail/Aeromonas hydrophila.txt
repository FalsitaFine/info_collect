
<i>Aeromonas hydrophila</i>: a type of bacteria commonly found in freshwater or brackish water and in some fish and shellfish. It may cause <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/dyspepsia/symptoms.htm">stomach upset</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/diarrhea/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">diarrhea</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/vomiting/symptoms.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="sym">vomiting</a> in some individuals if swallowed. It is more likely to cause intestinal symptoms in people with a suppressed immune system, such as people with <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cancer/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">cancer</a> or those who are receiving immune-suppressing medications. The bacteria may also enter the body through an open wound that becomes contaminated with the organisms. In 2012, a severe case of <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/necrotizing_fasciitis/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dt">necrotizing fasciitis</a> occurred in a young woman due to 
<i>Aeromonas hydrophila</i> infection, resulting in the loss of one leg.
