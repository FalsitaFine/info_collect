
Nasal polyps: Benign growths that originate in the mucous membranes lining the nasal passages or paranasal sinuses. Polyps are believed to arise in the nasal mucosa as a result of chronic inflammation. Up to 4% of adults have nasal polyps; they can also occur in children, particularly those with chronic respiratory conditions such as 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/cystic_fibrosis/article.htm">cystic fibrosis</a>. Symptoms include 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/chronic_rhinitis/article.htm">postnasal drainage</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/snoring/article.htm">snoring</a>, 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/headache/article.htm">headaches</a>,
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/sinusitis/article.htm">sinusitis</a>, and a reduced sense of smell and 
<a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/loss_of_taste_sensation/symptoms.htm">taste</a>. Therapy can include oral and topical steroid medications or surgical removal.
