
Amino acid, nonessential: An amino acid that can  be made by humans and so is not essential to the human diet. There are 11 nonessential <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=39555" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">amino acids</a>: alanine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=7006" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">arginine</a>, asparagine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22822" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">aspartic acid</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22823" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">cysteine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22824" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glutamic acid</a>, glutamine, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22826" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">glycine</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22832" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">proline</a>, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=22833" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="dict">serine</a>, and tyrosine.
