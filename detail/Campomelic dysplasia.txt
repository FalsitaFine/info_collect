
Campomelic dysplasia: A severe <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/birth_defects/article.htm" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="DT">birth defect</a> 
that affects the formation of the bones, genital organs, and facial features. 
The name "campomelic" comes from the Greek words meaning "bent limb." Physical features of the condition typically include bowing of the long bones of the legs and sometimes the arms, characteristic dimpling of the skin over the abnormal bones, shortened legs, underdeveloped shoulder blades, <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=14426" onclick="wmdTrack('embd-lnk');" rel="DICT">dislocated hips</a>, abnormally rotated feet (clubfeet), and an abnormal number of ribs. Distinctive facial features include a small chin, prominent eyes, and a flattened face. Maldevelopment of the airway in affected people can cause difficulty breathing.
