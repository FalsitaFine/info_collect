
Microchimerism: The presence of two genetically distinct and separately derived populations of cells, one population being at a low concentration, in the same individual or an organ such as the <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=2502">bone marrow</a>. Microchimerism may be due to transfer of cells between mother and fetus or between two twins. Other sources of microchimerism include blood transfusions and transplants. See also: <a href="https://www.medicinenet.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=8905">Chimera</a>.
